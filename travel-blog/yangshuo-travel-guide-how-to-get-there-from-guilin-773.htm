Yangshuo Travel Guide: How to Get There from Guilin

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Yangshuo, a picturesque county in Guangxi, is one of China’s most iconic travel destinations. Famous for its karst mountains, serene rivers, and vibrant culture, it’s a must-visit for adventurers, photographers, and anyone seeking a slice of rural China. If you’re starting your journey in Guilin, getting to Yangshuo is straightforward, with multiple transportation options. Here’s everything you need to know to make the trip seamless.

Best Ways to Travel from Guilin to Yangshuo

By Li River Cruise

One of the most scenic routes to Yangshuo is via a Li River cruise. This 4-5 hour journey takes you past breathtaking landscapes immortalized in Chinese art and literature.

  • Departure Point: Cruises typically leave from Zhujiang Pier (Guilin) around 9:00 AM.
  • Arrival: The cruise ends at Yangshuo’s Pier 1 or Xingping (some tours include a shuttle to Yangshuo).
  • Cost: Tickets range from $80–$150 USD, depending on the boat class.
  • Pro Tip: Book in advance during peak seasons (April–October).

By Bus

For budget travelers, buses are the most economical option.

  • Guilin Bus Station to Yangshuo: Buses run every 15–30 minutes from 6:30 AM to 8:30 PM.
  • Duration: 1.5–2 hours.
  • Cost: Around $4–$7 USD.
  • Drop-off: Yangshuo Bus Station, a short walk from West Street.

By Private Car or Taxi

If you prefer comfort and flexibility, hiring a private car is ideal.

  • Duration: 1–1.5 hours.
  • Cost: $40–$70 USD (negotiable).
  • Bonus: Stops at viewpoints like the Ten-Mile Gallery are possible.

By Electric Bike or Bicycle

For the adventurous, renting an e-bike or bicycle in Guilin and riding to Yangshuo is an unforgettable experience.

  • Route: Follow the Yulong River scenic path (about 50 km).
  • Time: 4–6 hours, depending on stops.
  • Rental Cost: $5–$15 USD per day.

Top Attractions in Yangshuo

Moon Hill

A natural arch with a crescent-shaped hole, Moon Hill offers panoramic views after a steep 20-minute hike.

  • Entrance Fee: $6 USD.
  • Best Time to Visit: Early morning or late afternoon to avoid crowds.

West Street (Xi Jie)

Yangshuo’s bustling pedestrian street is packed with cafes, bars, and souvenir shops.

  • Must-Try: Local beer fish and rice noodles.
  • Nightlife: Live music and rooftop bars light up after dark.

Yulong River Bamboo Rafting

A quieter alternative to the Li River, bamboo rafting here is peaceful and scenic.

  • Route: From Jinlong Bridge to Gongnong Bridge (1–2 hours).
  • Cost: $20–$30 USD per raft (seats 2).

Impression Sanjie Liu

A mesmerizing outdoor light show directed by Zhang Yimou, set against the Li River’s karst backdrop.

  • Showtimes: Evenings (weather permitting).
  • Tickets: $30–$50 USD.

Where to Stay in Yangshuo

Luxury: Yangshuo Mountain Retreat

  • Highlights: Riverside views, eco-friendly design.
  • Price: $100–$200 USD/night.

Mid-Range: Sudder Street Guesthouse

  • Highlights: West Street location, rooftop terrace.
  • Price: $40–$80 USD/night.

Budget: Yangshuo Backpacker Hostel

  • Highlights: Social atmosphere, bike rentals.
  • Price: $10–$20 USD/night.

Local Food You Can’t Miss

  • Beer Fish (Píjiǔ Yú): Fresh fish cooked in local beer.
  • Guilin Rice Noodles: A breakfast staple.
  • Stuffed Li River Snails: A spicy delicacy.

Seasonal Travel Tips

  • Spring (March–May): Ideal for cycling and photography.
  • Summer (June–August): Hot but great for river activities.
  • Autumn (September–November): Perfect weather for hiking.
  • Winter (December–February): Fewer tourists, misty landscapes.

Practical Tips for Your Trip

  • Cash: Some rural vendors don’t accept cards or mobile payments.
  • Language: Basic Mandarin or a translation app helps in remote areas.
  • Packing: Comfortable shoes for hiking and a rain jacket (unpredictable weather).

Yangshuo’s charm lies in its blend of natural beauty and laid-back vibe. Whether you cruise the Li River or bike through rice paddies, the journey from Guilin is just the beginning of an unforgettable adventure.
