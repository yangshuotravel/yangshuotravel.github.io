Yangshuo Itinerary: Exploring the Local Art Scene

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Yangshuo, nestled in the heart of Guangxi’s karst landscape, is a dream destination for travelers seeking natural beauty and cultural immersion. While most visitors come for the iconic limestone peaks and Li River cruises, the town’s thriving art scene is an underrated gem. From street murals to indie galleries, Yangshuo’s creative spirit is as vibrant as its scenery. Here’s how to dive into the local art culture during your stay.

Day 1: Discovering Yangshuo’s Artistic Roots

Morning: West Street’s Hidden Murals

Start your art-focused itinerary with a stroll down West Street (Xi Jie), Yangshuo’s bustling pedestrian hub. Beyond the souvenir shops and cafés, keep an eye out for colorful murals adorning alleyways and building facades. Local and international artists have transformed blank walls into canvases, blending traditional Chinese motifs with modern street art. Don’t miss the mural near Mojo Café—a whimsical fusion of karst mountains and surreal figures.

Afternoon: Visit the Yangshuo Art Gallery

A short walk from West Street, the Yangshuo Art Gallery showcases works by regional artists. The gallery specializes in ink wash paintings (shui mo hua), capturing the ethereal beauty of the Li River and surrounding peaks. Look for pieces by Huang Ge, a Guilin-born painter whose landscapes evoke a dreamlike quality. The gallery also hosts rotating exhibitions, so check their schedule for live demonstrations or workshops.

Evening: Sunset Sketching at Moon Hill

Cap off your first day with a creative outdoor session. Hike or bike to Moon Hill, the natural arch framing the sky. Pack a sketchbook or watercolors and join the informal gathering of artists who set up easels here at golden hour. The play of light on the karsts is mesmerizing, and you might even pick up tips from local plein-air painters.

Day 2: Immersive Art Experiences

Morning: Bamboo Paper-Making Workshop

Venture to Xingping, a quieter riverside town 40 minutes from Yangshuo, to learn the ancient craft of bamboo paper-making. At Xingping Traditional Handicrafts Studio, artisans demonstrate how bamboo fibers are transformed into delicate sheets used for calligraphy and painting. Roll up your sleeves and try your hand at pressing pulp—it’s a tactile way to connect with China’s artistic heritage.

Afternoon: Cave Art & Indie Studios

Yangshuo’s art scene isn’t confined to conventional spaces. Book a guided tour to Silver Cave, where stalactites and stalagmites form a natural gallery. Local legends say the cave’s formations inspired early Zhuang minority rock paintings—ask your guide to point out the abstract patterns.

Back in town, seek out Liu Sanjie Art Space, an indie studio run by a collective of avant-garde artists. Their experimental installations often incorporate recycled materials, commenting on rural-urban dynamics. If you’re lucky, you’ll catch a live performance blending dance and visual art.

Evening: Artisan Markets & Live Music

As dusk falls, head to Yangshuo Park for the weekly Night Market of Makers. Vendors sell hand-painted fans, ceramic jewelry, and block-printed textiles. Strike up a conversation with Chen Xia, a printmaker whose work features mythical creatures from Guangxi folklore. Nearby, The Giggling Tree Café hosts acoustic sets by musician-artists—order a lychee kombucha and enjoy the fusion of sounds and visuals.

Day 3: Beyond the Canvas

Morning: Calligraphy with a Master

Art in Yangshuo isn’t just visual—it’s poetic. Join a calligraphy class at Bamboo House Cultural Center, where Master Wu teaches the elegance of kaishu (regular script). Learn how to balance ink density and brush pressure while crafting characters like “mountain” (山) and “water” (水)—symbols deeply tied to the region.

Afternoon: Eco-Art at the Yulong River

Rent a bamboo raft and float down the Yulong River, but with a twist: bring along biodegradable pigments to create river ephemeral art. This eco-conscious practice, inspired by Tibetan sand mandalas, involves arranging petals and colored rice into patterns on the riverbanks. Document your creation before the current gently washes it away—a meditation on impermanence.

Evening: Film & Folklore at Impression Sanjie Liu

End your journey with Impression Sanjie Liu, an open-air spectacle directed by Zhang Yimou. While not strictly “art” in the traditional sense, the show’s choreography and lighting transform the Li River into a living painting. The performance draws from Zhuang minority tales, with fishermen’s lanterns flickering like floating brushstrokes.

Yangshuo’s art scene is as layered as its topography. Whether you’re a collector, a doodler, or simply curious, these experiences reveal how creativity flourishes where nature and culture collide.
