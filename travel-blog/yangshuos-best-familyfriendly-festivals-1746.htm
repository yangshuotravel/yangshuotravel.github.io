Yangshuo’s Best Family-Friendly Festivals

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Yangshuo, with its breathtaking karst landscapes and vibrant culture, is not just a destination for adventurers and solo travelers—it’s also a fantastic place for families. Throughout the year, the town and its surroundings host a variety of festivals that cater to visitors of all ages. From traditional celebrations to modern events, these festivals offer a unique way to experience Yangshuo’s charm while keeping kids entertained. Here’s a guide to the best family-friendly festivals in Yangshuo.

1. The Yangshuo Spring Festival Celebrations

A Traditional Start to the Year

The Chinese New Year, or Spring Festival, is one of the most exciting times to visit Yangshuo. The town comes alive with red lanterns, fireworks, and lively performances. Families can enjoy:

  • Dragon and Lion Dances – Colorful parades fill the streets, with performers dancing to the beat of drums. Kids will love the energetic displays.
  • Lantern Making Workshops – Local artisans teach visitors how to craft traditional paper lanterns, a fun activity for children.
  • Special New Year’s Feasts – Many restaurants offer festive menus with dumplings (jiaozi) and other holiday treats.

Where to Experience It

The best spots are West Street (Xi Jie) and the town square, where most of the celebrations take place. Hotels and guesthouses often organize special events for families, including calligraphy lessons and storytelling sessions.

2. The Yangshuo International Music Festival

A Global Celebration of Sound

Held annually in October, this festival brings together musicians from around the world for a weekend of live performances. It’s a great way to introduce kids to different cultures through music.

  • Open-Air Concerts – Families can spread out on the grass and enjoy everything from folk to rock.
  • Interactive Workshops – Some events allow children to try instruments like the guzheng (Chinese zither) or African drums.
  • Street Performances – Buskers and small bands play along the Li River, creating a festive atmosphere.

Best for Older Kids and Teens

While younger children might enjoy the lively atmosphere, older kids and teens will appreciate the variety of music styles. The festival is held in multiple venues, including outdoor stages and cozy cafes.

3. The Moon Hill Climbing Festival

Adventure for the Whole Family

For active families who love the outdoors, the Moon Hill Climbing Festival (usually in April or May) is a must. This event celebrates Yangshuo’s reputation as a rock-climbing paradise.

  • Beginner-Friendly Routes – Guided climbs are available for kids and first-timers.
  • Scavenger Hunts – Some organizers set up fun challenges along the trails.
  • Picnic with a View – After the climb, families can relax at the top and enjoy the stunning karst scenery.

Safety First

Professional instructors ensure that even young climbers are safe. Helmets and harnesses are provided, making it a great introduction to the sport.

4. The Yangshuo Dragon Boat Festival

Racing on the Li River

Held in June, this festival coincides with the traditional Duanwu Festival. Teams compete in long, colorful boats while spectators cheer from the riverbanks.

  • Kid-Friendly Activities – Face painting, mini boat races, and traditional zongzi (sticky rice dumplings) tasting.
  • Cultural Performances – Folklore dances and drumming shows add to the excitement.
  • Riverside Picnics – Many families bring snacks and watch the races from the grassy banks.

Best Viewing Spots

The most action happens near the Big Banyan Tree area, where the river is wide enough for multiple boats to race side by side.

5. The Yangshuo Light Show

A Magical Nighttime Experience

This isn’t a traditional festival, but the nightly Impression Liu Sanjie show is a spectacle families shouldn’t miss. Set against the Li River, this outdoor performance uses water, light, and hundreds of performers to tell local legends.

  • Visual Spectacle – Kids will be mesmerized by the colorful costumes and floating stages.
  • Short and Engaging – The show lasts about an hour, perfect for young attention spans.
  • Evening Strolls – After the show, families can explore West Street’s night market for souvenirs and snacks.

Booking Tips

Tickets sell out fast, especially during peak seasons, so it’s best to book in advance. Some hotels offer package deals that include transportation.

6. The Yangshuo Harvest Festival

Celebrating Rural Life

In September, local farmers celebrate the harvest with markets, cooking competitions, and folk games. It’s a great way for kids to learn about rural Chinese traditions.

  • Farm Visits – Families can pick fruits like pomelos and lychees.
  • Cooking Classes – Learn to make local dishes like beer fish (a Yangshuo specialty).
  • Tug-of-War and Sack Races – Friendly competitions where kids can join in.

Where to Go

Villages like Xingping and Fuli host the most authentic celebrations, with fewer tourists and more hands-on activities.

7. The Yangshuo Hot Air Balloon Festival

Soaring Over the Karst Peaks

For a truly unforgettable experience, the hot air balloon festival (usually in November) offers family-friendly rides with panoramic views.

  • Tethered Flights – Safe for younger children, these short rides keep balloons close to the ground.
  • Balloon Glow Nights – In the evenings, illuminated balloons create a magical scene.
  • Photo Opportunities – The colorful balloons against the karst backdrop make for amazing family photos.

Best for Calm Weather

Since balloons depend on stable conditions, mornings are the best time for flights.

Yangshuo’s festivals offer something for every family—whether you’re into music, adventure, or cultural experiences. Each event provides a unique way to connect with the local culture while creating lasting memories.
