Yangshuo vs. Guilin: Where to Spend Golden Week?

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Golden Week is one of the most anticipated travel periods in China, offering a rare extended break for locals and international visitors alike. With stunning landscapes, rich culture, and endless adventure opportunities, Guangxi’s two most famous destinations—Guilin and Yangshuo—are top contenders for travelers. But which one should you pick for your Golden Week getaway? Let’s dive into the highlights, crowds, and hidden gems of each to help you decide.

The Scenic Showdown: Karst Peaks and Rivers

Guilin: The Classic Postcard View

Guilin is the gateway to Guangxi’s iconic karst scenery. The Li River cruise from Guilin to Yangshuo is a must-do, offering breathtaking views of limestone peaks shrouded in mist. Elephant Trunk Hill and Reed Flute Cave are other signature attractions, perfect for first-time visitors seeking that classic "Chinese landscape painting" vibe.

However, Golden Week means crowds. The Li River boats book up fast, and popular spots like Seven Star Park can feel overwhelming. If you prefer a quieter experience, consider early-morning visits or lesser-known spots like Daxu Ancient Town.

Yangshuo: Adventure and Laid-Back Vibes

Yangshuo takes Guilin’s scenery and dials up the adventure. The town itself is smaller and more backpacker-friendly, with a lively West Street (Xi Jie) full of cafes, bars, and souvenir shops. But the real magic lies outside town:

  • Bike rides through rice paddies: Rent a bike and explore the Yulong River valley, passing water buffaloes and bamboo groves.
  • Rock climbing: Yangshuo is a global hotspot for climbers, with routes for all skill levels.
  • Bamboo rafting: Skip the crowded Li River and opt for a peaceful float down the Yulong.

Golden Week here is busy but manageable—most crowds concentrate in Xi Jie, while the countryside remains relatively serene.

Cultural Experiences: Tradition vs. Tourism

Guilin’s Historical Roots

Guilin boasts deeper historical ties, with sites like:
- Sun and Moon Pagodas: Lit up beautifully at night.
- Ancient South Gate: A remnant of the Ming Dynasty.
- Local opera and tea houses: For a taste of traditional Guangxi culture.

That said, commercialization is rampant. Expect inflated prices and tourist traps during peak season.

Yangshuo’s Fusion Vibe

Yangshuo’s culture is a mix of local traditions and expat influences. Highlights include:
- Impression Sanjie Liu: A stunning outdoor light show directed by Zhang Yimou, set against the Li River.
- Cooking classes: Learn to make Guilin rice noodles or beer fish (a local specialty).
- Village homestays: Escape the tourist zone by staying in nearby villages like Xingping.

Food Face-Off: Where to Feast

Guilin’s Classic Dishes

Guilin is the birthplace of Guilin mifen (rice noodles), best enjoyed at local joints like Shi Hui Mifen. Other must-tries:
- Beer fish (though Yangshuo does it better).
- Osmanthus cake: A sweet treat made from local flowers.

Dining in Guilin can be hit-or-miss during Golden Week—popular spots have long waits, so explore side streets for hidden gems.

Yangshuo’s Eclectic Eats

Yangshuo’s food scene is more diverse, thanks to its backpacker crowd. Don’t miss:
- West Street’s international cafes: Pizza, burgers, and even Mexican food.
- Riverside BBQ: Freshly grilled fish by the Yulong.
- Farm-to-table restaurants: Organic produce from nearby villages.

For authenticity, head to Xingping for the best beer fish away from the crowds.

Crowds and Logistics: Surviving Golden Week

Guilin’s Urban Hustle

Pros:
- Better transport links (airport, high-speed rail).
- More hotel options, from luxury to budget.

Cons:
- Overcrowded attractions.
- Higher prices for tours and taxis.

Tip: Book Li River cruises months in advance.

Yangshuo’s Chill Chaos

Pros:
- Easier to escape crowds (rent a scooter and explore).
- Cheaper hostels and guesthouses.

Cons:
- Limited public transport (buses to/from Guilin get packed).
- Xi Jie turns into a tourist zoo at night.

Tip: Stay in Fuli or Xingping for a quieter base.

Hidden Gems: Off-the-Beaten-Path

Guilin’s Secret Spots

  • Daxu Ancient Town: A 600-year-old market town.
  • Yao Mountain: For panoramic views sans crowds.

Yangshuo’s Untouched Beauty

  • Moon Hill: Hike for epic sunset views.
  • Longji Rice Terraces: A day trip to these stunning fields is worth the drive.

Verdict: Who Wins Golden Week?

For first-timers or families, Guilin offers convenience and iconic sights—just brace for crowds. For adventurers and solo travelers, Yangshuo’s mix of nature and nightlife is unbeatable.

No matter your pick, book early, rise at dawn, and venture beyond the postcard spots. Golden Week is chaotic, but with the right strategy, Guangxi’s magic shines through.
