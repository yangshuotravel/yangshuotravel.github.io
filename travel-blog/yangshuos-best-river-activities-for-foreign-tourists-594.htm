Yangshuo’s Best River Activities for Foreign Tourists

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Yangshuo, nestled in the heart of Guangxi’s karst landscape, is a dream destination for travelers seeking adventure, relaxation, and breathtaking scenery. The Li River and Yulong River are the lifeblood of this region, offering unforgettable experiences for foreign tourists. Whether you’re into kayaking, bamboo rafting, or simply soaking in the views, Yangshuo’s river activities are a must-try. Here’s your ultimate guide to the best water-based adventures in this iconic Chinese destination.

The Li River: A Scenic Wonderland

The Li River is the crown jewel of Yangshuo, famous for its emerald waters and towering limestone peaks. A cruise or kayak trip here is like gliding through a traditional Chinese painting.

1. Li River Cruise from Guilin to Yangshuo

For a relaxed yet immersive experience, the 4-hour Li River cruise from Guilin to Yangshuo is unbeatable. You’ll pass iconic landmarks like Xingping Village (featured on the 20 RMB note) and the Nine Horses Fresco Hill. Opt for a luxury boat for added comfort or a local ferry for an authentic vibe.

Pro Tip: Book a morning cruise to avoid crowds and catch the misty river views—perfect for photography!

2. Kayaking the Li River

If you prefer an active adventure, kayaking lets you explore hidden coves and quiet stretches of the river. Many tour operators offer guided kayak trips, ensuring safety while allowing flexibility to stop at riverside villages.

Hotspot: The section between Yangdi and Xingping is less touristy and offers stunning karst formations.

The Yulong River: Serenity and Adventure Combined

Smaller and quieter than the Li River, the Yulong is ideal for bamboo rafting, cycling along its banks, and even stand-up paddleboarding (SUP).

1. Bamboo Rafting on the Yulong River

A quintessential Yangshuo experience, bamboo rafting is peaceful and picturesque. The rafts are hand-poled by local operators, drifting past rice paddies and water buffalo.

Best Routes:
- Jima to Gongnong Bridge (60-90 mins): A classic route with gentle currents and postcard-worthy scenery.
- Dragon Bridge to Jiuxian Village (2 hours): Longer and more secluded, perfect for nature lovers.

Note: Some rafts have small motors for convenience, but the traditional silent rafts are more eco-friendly.

2. Cycling Along the Yulong River

Rent a bike in Yangshuo and follow the flat, well-marked paths beside the Yulong. You’ll pass ancient bridges, farmers’ fields, and hidden swimming spots.

Must-See Stop: The iconic Yulong Bridge, a 600-year-old stone arch bridge, is a great photo op.

3. Stand-Up Paddleboarding (SUP)

For a fun twist, try SUP on the Yulong’s calm waters. Local outfitters provide lessons and tours, often including cliff jumping spots.

Best Time: Early morning or late afternoon to avoid the midday heat.

Hidden Gems: Lesser-Known River Experiences

Beyond the Li and Yulong, Yangshuo has secret spots waiting to be discovered.

1. Moon Water Cave’s Underground River

Dare to explore this cave system near the Yulong River, where you can wade or swim through crystal-clear underground streams. Guided tours include headlamps and safety gear.

2. Fishing with Locals

Join a fisherman on a traditional cormorant fishing boat (though now mostly for show) or try your hand at bamboo rod fishing in quieter river sections.

Practical Tips for Foreign Tourists

  • Best Seasons: Spring (March-May) and autumn (September-November) offer mild weather and clear skies.
  • Safety: Always wear life jackets during water activities; currents can be unpredictable.
  • Eco-Friendly Travel: Avoid littering and support local businesses that prioritize sustainability.
  • Language Barrier: Download a translation app or carry a phrasebook—many operators speak basic English.

Yangshuo’s rivers are more than just backdrops; they’re gateways to adventure, culture, and tranquility. Whether you’re paddling through karst peaks or drifting under ancient bridges, these experiences will leave you with memories as timeless as the landscape itself.
