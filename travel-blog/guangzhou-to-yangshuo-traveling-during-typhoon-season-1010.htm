Guangzhou to Yangshuo: Traveling During Typhoon Season

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Traveling from Guangzhou to Yangshuo is an adventure filled with stunning landscapes, vibrant culture, and unforgettable experiences. However, if your trip coincides with typhoon season (typically May to October), you’ll need to be prepared for unpredictable weather. This guide will help you navigate the challenges while still making the most of your journey.

Understanding Typhoon Season in Southern China

Typhoons are a common occurrence in southern China, particularly in coastal regions like Guangdong and Guangxi. These powerful storms bring heavy rain, strong winds, and occasional flooding, which can disrupt travel plans.

When Is Typhoon Season?

The peak months for typhoons are July to September, though storms can occur as early as May and as late as October. If you’re planning a trip during this time, staying informed about weather forecasts is crucial.

How Typhoons Affect Travel

  • Flight and train delays/cancellations – Major storms often disrupt transportation.
  • Road closures – Heavy rain can cause landslides or flooding on highways.
  • Attraction closures – Some outdoor sites, like the Li River cruises, may temporarily shut down.

Preparing for Your Trip

1. Check Weather Forecasts Regularly

Before leaving Guangzhou, monitor updates from reliable sources like the China Meteorological Administration or international weather apps. If a typhoon is approaching, adjust your itinerary accordingly.

2. Pack Smart

  • Waterproof gear – A sturdy rain jacket, waterproof shoes, and a dry bag for electronics are essential.
  • Quick-dry clothing – Humidity is high, so lightweight, moisture-wicking fabrics work best.
  • Emergency supplies – Power banks, bottled water, and snacks in case of delays.

3. Flexible Booking Options

Book refundable flights, trains, and hotels. Many platforms like Ctrip (Trip.com) offer free cancellations if a typhoon warning is issued.

The Journey: Guangzhou to Yangshuo

Option 1: High-Speed Train (Best for Typhoon Season)

The Guangzhou South to Yangshuo high-speed train takes about 2.5 hours and is less likely to be canceled than flights. However, severe weather can still cause delays.

Pros:
- Faster than buses.
- More reliable during heavy rain.

Cons:
- Limited luggage space.
- May require a taxi/bus transfer to reach Yangshuo’s scenic areas.

Option 2: Flights (Riskier During Storms)

Flying from Guangzhou Baiyun Airport to Guilin Liangjiang Airport is quick (1.5 hours), but typhoons frequently cause cancellations.

Pros:
- Fastest option in good weather.

Cons:
- High chance of disruption.
- Additional travel time from Guilin to Yangshuo (1.5-hour drive).

Option 3: Long-Distance Bus (Cheapest but Least Reliable)

Buses are affordable but prone to delays due to flooded roads. If you choose this option, check road conditions beforehand.

Exploring Yangshuo During Typhoon Season

Even with stormy weather, Yangshuo offers plenty to do. Here’s how to enjoy your trip rain or shine.

Indoor Activities

1. Cooking Classes

Learn to make Guilin rice noodles or beer fish, a local specialty. Many cooking schools, like Yangshuo Cooking School, offer hands-on experiences.

2. Calligraphy & Painting Workshops

Traditional Chinese art classes are a great way to immerse yourself in culture while staying dry.

3. Tea Tasting

Visit a teahouse and sample osmanthus tea or jasmine tea, both popular in Guangxi.

Outdoor Adventures (Weather Permitting)

1. Li River Cruise (If Operating)

The iconic cruise from Xingping to Yangshuo is breathtaking, but heavy rain can make the river unsafe. Check with local operators before booking.

2. Cycling Through the Countryside

If the rain lets up, rent a bike and explore Yulong River Valley. The misty karst mountains look even more dramatic in stormy weather.

3. Visit Silver Cave

This massive limestone cave is a perfect rainy-day alternative. The illuminated formations are mesmerizing.

Staying Safe During a Typhoon

1. Follow Local Alerts

If a typhoon warning is issued, stay indoors. Avoid riverside areas due to flash flood risks.

2. Have a Backup Plan

If outdoor activities are canceled, switch to indoor alternatives like museums or cafes.

3. Know Emergency Contacts

Save the number for local police (110) and medical services (120) in case of emergencies.

Final Thoughts

Traveling from Guangzhou to Yangshuo during typhoon season requires extra planning, but it’s still worth the trip. With the right preparation, you can enjoy the region’s beauty while staying safe. Whether you’re sipping tea in a cozy teahouse or marveling at the mist-covered karsts, Yangshuo’s magic shines through—even in the rain.
