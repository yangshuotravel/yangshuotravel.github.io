Best Hostels in Yangshuo for Budget Travelers

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Yangshuo, nestled in the heart of Guangxi’s karst mountains, is a backpacker’s paradise. With its breathtaking landscapes, vibrant nightlife, and endless outdoor adventures, it’s no wonder budget travelers flock here. But where should you stay to make the most of your trip without breaking the bank? We’ve rounded up the best hostels in Yangshuo that offer affordability, comfort, and a killer social scene.

Why Stay in a Hostel in Yangshuo?

Hostels aren’t just about saving money—they’re about experiences. In Yangshuo, hostels often double as social hubs, organizing bike tours, rock-climbing trips, and even cooking classes. Plus, they’re the perfect place to meet fellow travelers and swap stories over a cold beer.

Top Picks for Budget-Friendly Hostels

Here are the best hostels in Yangshuo that combine great value with unforgettable vibes.

1. Yangshuo Sudder Street Hostel

Located just a short walk from West Street, Sudder Street is a favorite among backpackers. The rooftop bar offers stunning views of the karst peaks, and the staff organizes daily activities like sunrise hikes and bamboo rafting trips.

  • Why Stay Here?
    • Free bike rentals
    • On-site bar with happy hour deals
    • Cozy dorm rooms with privacy curtains

2. River View Hostel

True to its name, this hostel sits right by the Li River, offering some of the best sunrise views in town. It’s a bit quieter than the downtown spots, making it ideal for travelers who want a mix of relaxation and adventure.

  • Why Stay Here?
    • Private riverside terrace
    • Affordable private rooms
    • Easy access to cycling routes

3. Wada Hostel

A stylish boutique hostel with a minimalist design, Wada is perfect for travelers who appreciate aesthetics without the high price tag. The common area is a great place to meet people, and the staff can hook you up with discounted rock-climbing tours.

  • Why Stay Here?
    • Instagram-worthy interiors
    • Free breakfast included
    • Walking distance to local markets

What to Look for in a Yangshuo Hostel

Not all hostels are created equal. Here’s what to consider before booking:

Location, Location, Location

Staying near West Street means you’ll be in the middle of the action—perfect for nightlife lovers. If you prefer peace and nature, look for hostels closer to Xingping or the Yulong River.

Social Atmosphere

Some hostels are party-centric, while others focus on relaxation. Check reviews to see if the vibe matches your travel style.

Amenities That Matter

  • Free Wi-Fi (non-negotiable for most travelers)
  • Bike Rentals (the best way to explore Yangshuo)
  • Organized Tours (saves you the hassle of planning)

Hidden Gems: Off-the-Beaten-Path Hostels

If you’re willing to venture a little further, these lesser-known spots offer unique experiences.

1. Moon Hill Hostel

Tucked near the iconic Moon Hill, this hostel is a haven for hikers. The owners are avid climbers and can arrange private guides for off-the-grid trails.

2. Rice Fields Hostel

Surrounded by terraced rice fields, this eco-friendly hostel is a serene escape. They even offer farm-to-table dinners using local ingredients.

Tips for Booking the Best Hostel

  • Book in Advance – Yangshuo gets busy, especially during peak seasons.
  • Read Recent Reviews – Hostels can change management quickly.
  • Ask About Discounts – Some places offer deals for longer stays.

Final Thoughts

Whether you’re here to climb limestone peaks, cruise the Li River, or just soak in the laid-back vibes, Yangshuo’s hostels provide the perfect base for your adventures. From riverside retreats to social hubs, there’s something for every type of budget traveler. So pack your bags, grab a bike, and get ready to explore one of China’s most stunning destinations—without emptying your wallet.
