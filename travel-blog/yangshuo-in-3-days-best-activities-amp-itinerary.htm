Yangshuo in 3 Days: Best Activities & Itinerary

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

The name Yangshuo conjures images of emerald-green rivers snaking through a dreamscape of karst pinnacles, their jagged peaks piercing the mist. This slice of Guangxi, immortalized on the 20 RMB note and in countless travelogues, is more than a postcard—it’s a full-sensory experience. While it’s a staple on the China backpacker trail, its magic is far from diluted. This 3-day itinerary is designed to move you beyond the tourist hubs of West Street, weaving together heart-pumping adventure, profound cultural moments, and those serene, picture-perfect vistas that make Yangshuo a global icon. Forget rushing; here, the philosophy is to slow down and absorb the landscape that has inspired poets and painters for centuries.

Day 1: Rivers, Peaks, and the Pulse of West Street

Your Yangshuo immersion begins not on land, but on water. The Li River (Li Jiang) is the region’s lifeblood, and to understand Yangshuo, you must first glide upon it.

Morning: A Li River Bamboo Raft Journey

Head early to the dock at Xingping Town, about a 45-minute drive from Yangshuo town. This is where you’ll find the most iconic and less-crowded section of the river. Aboard a motorized bamboo raft (the traditional, poled rafts are no longer used for these main routes), you’ll embark on a living painting. Each bend reveals a new masterpiece: water buffalo cooling off in the shallows, fishermen with their trained cormorants (though now mostly for photos), and the majestic peaks with names like "Nine Horses Fresco Hill" and "Yellow Cloth Shoal." This is the exact view featured on Chinese currency. Pro tip: Bring a 20 RMB note to compare the real thing to the engraving. The quiet hum of the raft’s motor allows you to fully soak in the tranquility and scale of the scenery.

Afternoon: Conquering Xianggong Mountain

After returning to shore, a short but steep hike awaits. Xianggong Mountain, located between Xingping and Yangshuo, offers arguably the most breathtaking panoramic view in all of Guilin. The 20-minute climb up stone steps is rewarded with a 360-degree spectacle. On a clear day, you’ll see the Li River winding like a jade ribbon through a boundless forest of karst towers—a view that defines "epic." It’s a photographer’s paradise, especially at sunrise or sunset, but even in the afternoon light, the vista is unforgettable.

Evening: West Street Exploration & Culinary Delights

Return to Yangshuo town and dive into the controlled chaos of West Street (Xi Jie). This ancient street, now a vibrant fusion of Chinese and Western influences, pulses with energy. Browse for souvenirs like silk scarves or minority embroidery, but the real attraction is the food. Your mission tonight is to taste local Guilin cuisine. Start with the world-famous Guilin Mifen (rice noodles) in a savory, slightly spicy broth, topped with peanuts, pickled vegetables, and your choice of meat. Then, seek out Yangshuo Beer Fish—fresh river fish braised in local beer, tomatoes, and chili—a tangy, flavorful dish born here. End the night with a craft beer at a rooftop bar, the surreal karst silhouettes visible against the twilight sky.

Day 2: Countryside Cycling, Cultural Gems, and a Legendary Show

Today, trade the river for rural paths and immerse yourself in the slower, agricultural heartbeat of the Yangshuo basin.

Morning: Bike Ride Through the Yulong River Valley

Rent a bicycle or an electric scooter (a fantastic option for covering more ground effortlessly) and set out for the Yulong River area. This is the essence of Yangshuo’s countryside. Pedal along flat, paved paths flanked by rice paddies, ancient stone bridges, and grazing water buffalo. The iconic Yulong Bridge, a centuries-old stone arch bridge, is a perfect rest stop. The real adventure? Taking a bamboo raft on the Yulong River. Unlike the Li River, these are often the genuine, manually-poled rafts. The trip is quieter, more intimate, and you can even experience going over small dams, which adds a splash of excitement. It’s a serene and utterly charming experience.

Afternoon: Silver Cave and Village Life

After a rustic lunch at a farmer’s house (try stuffed Li River snails), head to the magnificent Silver Cave (Silver Yan). This colossal karst cave system is a subterranean wonderland. Well-lit walkways guide you past stunning formations—stalactites, stalagmites, stone waterfalls, and crystalline pools—all given imaginative names. The scale is immense and offers a cool respite from the afternoon heat. On your way back, detour through a working village like Jiuxian or Litou to see daily rural life far from the tourist track.

Evening: Impression Sanjie Liu

As night falls, prepare for a cultural spectacle unlike any other. Impression Sanjie Liu is a monumental open-air theater production directed by famed filmmaker Zhang Yimou. Its stage is the very Li River and its karst backdrop; its ceiling, the sky. Over 600 local performers from the Zhuang, Yao, Miao, and Dong ethnic groups use the natural landscape as part of the set, with lights, song, and movement telling folk stories. The use of fishing boats, reflective water, and the illuminated peaks is sheer magic. It’s a testament to the deep cultural roots of this visually stunning region. Book tickets well in advance.

Day 3: Adventure, Refinement, and a Farewell from Above

Your final day amps up the adrenaline before offering a moment of peaceful reflection and a grand finale.

Morning: Choose Your Thrill - Rock Climbing or River Tubing

Yangshuo is a world-renowned rock climbing destination, with over 400 bolted routes for all skill levels. Beginners can take a half-day course with one of the many reputable outfitters in town, scaling a real karst peak with expert guidance. The view from the wall is unbeatable. Prefer water-based fun? Head to the nearby Dragon River (Long Jing) for a thrilling river tubing or drifting experience. Float through serene sections and navigate mild rapids, surrounded by lush greenery and towering cliffs. Both activities offer an active, exhilarating connection to the landscape.

Afternoon: Tea Plantation Visit and a Cooking Class

Slow the pace with a visit to one of Yangshuo’s tranquil tea plantations in the foothills. Learn about the tea-making process, from leaf picking to drying and rolling, and participate in a traditional tea ceremony. Sipping locally grown osmanthus tea or jasmine tea while overlooking terraced fields is a moment of pure Zen. Later, consider joining a Chinese cooking class. Many local schools offer market tours to select fresh ingredients before teaching you to craft classics like Beer Fish or Kung Pao Chicken. It’s the perfect souvenir—a skill you can take home.

Late Afternoon: A Hot Air Balloon or Paragliding Ride

For your farewell to Yangshuo, gain a bird’s-eye perspective. A hot air balloon ride at dawn or dusk provides a silent, majestic float over the karst forest, a truly bucket-list experience. For more dynamism, try paragliding. Tandem flights launch from mountains like Feng Lou Shan (Bee Mountain), offering 15-20 minutes of soaring on thermal currents with unparalleled, sweeping views of the river valleys and peaks below. It’s an unforgettable, freeing way to etch the grandeur of Yangshuo into your memory before you depart.

Yangshuo’s allure lies in its perfect balance. It caters to the thrill-seeker and the serenity-seeker with equal grace. In just three days, you can touch its ancient cultural veins, feel the rush of its modern adventures, and forever carry with you the vision of those timeless green peaks rising from the mist. The itinerary is full, but the feeling it leaves is one of deep, rejuvenating peace.
