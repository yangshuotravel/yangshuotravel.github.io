Yangshuo’s Best Day Trips for Solo Explorers

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Yangshuo, nestled in Guangxi’s karst landscape, is a dream destination for solo travelers. With its towering limestone peaks, winding rivers, and vibrant culture, it offers endless opportunities for adventure, relaxation, and self-discovery. Whether you’re into hiking, cycling, or simply soaking in the scenery, here are the best day trips for solo explorers in Yangshuo.

1. Cycling the Yulong River Valley

Why It’s Perfect for Solo Travelers

Cycling along the Yulong River is one of the most rewarding ways to experience Yangshuo’s countryside. The flat terrain makes it accessible for all fitness levels, and the route is dotted with bamboo groves, rice paddies, and quaint villages.

Must-See Stops

  • Dragon Bridge (Yulongqiao) – A historic stone bridge perfect for photos.
  • Bamboo Rafting – Take a break and float down the river on a traditional bamboo raft.
  • Local Farms – Stop by a roadside stall for fresh fruit or sugarcane juice.

Pro Tip

Rent a bike from Yangshuo town (around ¥30/day) and start early to avoid the midday heat.

2. Hiking Xianggong Hill for Sunrise

Why It’s a Solo Traveler’s Must-Do

For those who love solitude and breathtaking views, Xianggong Hill offers the best sunrise panorama of the Li River and karst mountains. The hike is short (about 20 minutes) but steep, making it manageable yet rewarding.

What to Expect

  • 360-Degree Views – The summit overlooks the river’s dramatic bends.
  • Fewer Crowds – Unlike more touristy spots, Xianggong remains relatively peaceful.

Logistics

  • Getting There – Hire a taxi or join a sunrise tour from Yangshuo.
  • Timing – Arrive at least 30 minutes before sunrise for the best light.

3. Exploring Xingping’s Old Town & 20 Yuan View

Why Solo Travelers Love It

Xingping is a charming riverside town with ancient streets and the famous "20 Yuan view" (the scene printed on China’s ¥20 bill). It’s less commercialized than Yangshuo, offering a more authentic experience.

Highlights

  • 20 Yuan Photo Spot – Snap your own version of the iconic landscape.
  • Old Street – Wander through Ming and Qing dynasty architecture.
  • Local Cuisine – Try beer fish (píjiǔ yú), a regional specialty.

Getting There

Take a bus from Yangshuo (about 1 hour) or book a private boat tour for a scenic route.

4. Rock Climbing at Moon Hill or Swiss Cheese Rock

Why It’s Great for Solo Adventurers

Yangshuo is a global rock-climbing hub, with routes for all skill levels. Whether you’re a beginner or an expert, climbing solo is a fantastic way to challenge yourself while meeting fellow travelers.

Best Spots

  • Moon Hill – Iconic for its crescent-shaped arch and beginner-friendly routes.
  • Swiss Cheese Rock – Named for its holes, offering varied climbs.

How to Do It Safely

  • Join a Guided Tour – Many hostels and climbing shops offer half-day excursions.
  • Rent Gear – If experienced, hire equipment and climb independently.

5. Bamboo Rafting on the Li River

Why It’s Ideal for Solo Relaxation

A peaceful bamboo raft ride lets you unwind while taking in Yangshuo’s most famous scenery. The gentle pace is perfect for solo travelers looking to reflect and enjoy nature.

Best Sections

  • Yangdi to Xingping – The most scenic stretch, passing the Nine Horses Fresco Hill.
  • Yulong River – A quieter alternative with fewer motorized boats.

Booking Tips

  • Shared vs. Private Rafts – Shared rafts are cheaper, but private ones offer more solitude.
  • Avoid Peak Hours – Go early morning or late afternoon for fewer crowds.

6. Visiting the Silver Cave (Yinzi Yan)

Why It’s Worth the Trip

This massive limestone cave is a surreal underground world of stalactites and illuminated rock formations. It’s a great escape from the heat and a unique solo exploration.

What to See Inside

  • Music Hall – A cavern with natural acoustics.
  • Golden Falls – A shimmering waterfall-like rock formation.

Practical Info

  • Distance from Yangshuo – About 30 minutes by scooter or taxi.
  • Tour Duration – Allow 1.5-2 hours for the full walkthrough.

7. Learning Cooking at a Local Farm

Why Solo Foodies Should Try It

Yangshuo’s cooking classes are a fun way to dive into Guangxi’s cuisine while meeting other travelers. Many include a market tour and hands-on cooking session.

What You’ll Learn

  • Stir-Fried Li River Snails – A local delicacy.
  • Guilin Rice Noodles – A must-try breakfast dish.

Best Classes

  • Yangshuo Cooking School – Highly rated for its farm-to-table approach.
  • Local Family Workshops – More intimate and personalized.

8. Nightlife & West Street (Xi Jie) Exploration

Why It’s Fun for Solo Socializers

West Street is the heart of Yangshuo’s nightlife, packed with bars, live music, and international eateries. It’s a great place to meet fellow backpackers.

Top Spots

  • Mojo Bar – Famous for its reggae nights.
  • Lucy’s Café – A backpacker hub with great Western and Chinese food.

Safety Tips

  • Stay Aware – Like any tourist area, watch your belongings.
  • Make Friends – Hostel bars are great for meeting other solo travelers.

Yangshuo is a paradise for solo explorers, blending adventure, culture, and relaxation. Whether you’re cycling through rice fields, climbing karst peaks, or savoring local flavors, every day brings a new discovery. Pack light, stay curious, and let Yangshuo’s magic guide your journey.
