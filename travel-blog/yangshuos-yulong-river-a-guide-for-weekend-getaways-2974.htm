Yangshuo’s Yulong River: A Guide for Weekend Getaways

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

The moment you arrive in Yangshuo, it feels like stepping into a classical Chinese painting. The dramatic karst peaks rise from the earth like nature’s own skyscrapers, draped in mist and mystery. While the Li River often steals the spotlight, it’s the Yulong River—its quieter, more intimate cousin—that offers the perfect escape for a weekend retreat. Less crowded, more serene, and equally stunning, the Yulong River is where you go to truly connect with the soul of Guangxi.

Why Yulong River Should Be Your Next Weekend Destination

Unlike the bustling Li River, which is often packed with large tourist boats, the Yulong River maintains a tranquil, almost secretive charm. It’s a place where time slows down. The river meanders through idyllic countryside, past ancient stone bridges, lush bamboo groves, and small villages where life moves at the rhythm of the seasons. For those seeking a break from urban chaos, this is the ultimate antidote.

Eco-Tourism and Sustainable Travel

One of the biggest travel trends right now is sustainable tourism, and the Yulong River is at the forefront of this movement in Yangshuo. Local operators are increasingly focusing on eco-friendly practices, from bamboo rafting tours that leave no trace to bike rentals that encourage low-impact exploration. Travelers today don’t just want to see beauty—they want to preserve it. Here, you can enjoy nature responsibly while supporting the local economy.

Must-Do Activities Along the Yulong River

Bamboo Rafting: The Iconic Experience

No trip to the Yulong River is complete without a bamboo raft ride. These handmade rafts, poled by local experts, glide silently down the gentle currents. The experience is meditative—you’ll pass under the ancient Fuli Bridge, watch water buffalo grazing along the banks, and see fishermen practicing traditional cormorant fishing (though mostly for show these days, it’s still fascinating). For the perfect weekend activity, opt for a morning or late afternoon ride when the light is soft and the crowds are thin.

Cycling the Countryside Trails

Rent a bike in Yangshuo town and spend a day exploring the riverside paths. The flat terrain makes it accessible for all fitness levels, and the views are nothing short of spectacular. You’ll pedal through farmland, past karst formations, and over small stone bridges. Stop at a local farmhouse for a freshly cooked lunch—try the beer fish, a Yangshuo specialty—or simply enjoy a picnic by the water.

Hiking to Hidden Viewpoints

For the more adventurous, hiking trails around the Yulong River offer some of the best views in Guangxi. While the Xianggong Hill viewpoint near the Li River is famous, the trails around the Yulong area are less crowded and equally rewarding. Climb to the top of Moon Hill for a panoramic vista, or explore the narrow paths that lead to secluded spots where you can have the landscape all to yourself.

Village Hopping and Cultural immersion

The villages along the Yulong River, such as Jiuxian and Yulong Bridge Village, offer a glimpse into rural Chinese life. Many families run small guesthouses or cafes where you can relax with a cup of tea and chat with locals. This is also where you can see traditional crafts like fan-making or pottery. It’s a chance to experience the culture beyond the tourist sites.

Where to Stay: From Rustic to Luxury

Riverside Boutique Hotels

In recent years, Yangshuo has seen a surge in stylish boutique hotels that blend modern comfort with traditional design. Properties like the Yangshuo Mountain Retreat or the Banyan Tree Yangshuo offer rooms with breathtaking river views, private balconies, and amenities like spas and fine dining. These are perfect for travelers who want to immerse themselves in nature without sacrificing luxury.

Traditional Guesthouses and Homestays

For a more authentic experience, book a room in a family-run guesthouse. You’ll get home-cooked meals, personal recommendations, and a chance to connect with the local community. Many of these homestays are located in ancient villages, adding to the charm.

Glamping and Eco-Lodges

Glamping (glamorous camping) is another hot trend here. Imagine sleeping in a luxury tent under the stars, with the karst peaks silhouetted against the night sky. Several operators now offer glamping experiences along the Yulong River, complete with bonfires, gourmet meals, and guided nature walks.

Foodie Adventures: What to Eat and Drink

Yangshuo’s food scene is a highlight of any visit. The cuisine is a mix of Guilin flavors and local specialties, with an emphasis on fresh, seasonal ingredients.

Must-Try Dishes

Start with Yangshuo beer fish—fresh river fish cooked in local beer with tomatoes and chili. It’s tangy, slightly spicy, and utterly delicious. Also, don’t miss Guilin rice noodles, often served for breakfast with peanuts, pickled vegetables, and a rich broth. For something unique, try stuffed Li River snails or taro pork.

Riverside Dining

Several restaurants along the Yulong River offer outdoor seating with stunning views. Enjoy a meal while listening to the sound of flowing water and watching the sunset paint the karst peaks in shades of orange and pink. It’s a romantic and unforgettable experience.

Local Markets and Street Food

Visit Yangshuo’s West Street or the local morning market to sample street food like oil tea, sticky rice dumplings, or grilled skewers. It’s a feast for the senses and a great way to dive into the local culture.

Photography Tips: Capturing the Magic

The Yulong River is a photographer’s dream. The ever-changing light, reflected in the water and dancing on the karst peaks, creates endless opportunities.

Best Times for Shooting

Sunrise and sunset are magical here. The soft golden light enhances the dramatic landscape, and the mist often rises from the river in the early morning, adding a dreamy quality. For the best shots, head to the Yulong Bridge or find a high vantage point along the hiking trails.

Drone Photography

If you have a drone, this is the place to use it. The aerial views of the river winding through the karst formations are incredible. Just be sure to follow local regulations and fly responsibly.

Practical Tips for Your Weekend Trip

Getting There

Yangshuo is easily accessible from major cities like Guilin or Guangzhou. The high-speed train to Guilin takes about 2.5 hours from Guangzhou, followed by a bus or taxi to Yangshuo. Alternatively, you can fly to Guilin Liangjiang International Airport and then take a shuttle.

Best Time to Visit

Spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November) are ideal, with pleasant temperatures and clear skies. Summer can be hot and crowded, while winter is quieter but cooler.

What to Pack

Comfortable walking shoes, lightweight clothing, a rain jacket (weather can be unpredictable), sunscreen, and insect repellent. Don’t forget your camera!

Language and Communication

While English is spoken in tourist areas, learning a few basic Mandarin phrases can enhance your experience. Phrases like "nǐ hǎo" (hello) and "xiè xie" (thank you) go a long way.

Beyond the Weekend: Extending Your Stay

If you have extra time, explore more of Yangshuo. Take a cooking class to learn how to make local dishes, try rock climbing on the karst peaks (a growing adventure sport here), or visit the Impression Liu Sanjie light show directed by Zhang Yimou, which takes place on the Li River and is a spectacle of music, light, and culture.

The Yulong River is more than just a destination—it’s an experience that stays with you long after you leave. Whether you’re drifting on a bamboo raft, cycling through emerald fields, or simply soaking in the views, this corner of China offers a perfect blend of adventure, relaxation, and cultural immersion.
