Best Restaurants for a Rainy Day in Yangshuo

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Yangshuo, with its dramatic karst landscapes and serene Li River, is a dream destination for travelers. But what happens when the skies open up and rain transforms the town into a misty wonderland? While outdoor adventures might take a pause, the culinary scene comes alive. Cozy up in one of these fantastic restaurants, where the ambiance, food, and warmth make a rainy day in Yangshuo just as memorable.

Cozy Cafés with Stunning Views

1. Cloud 9 Café

Nestled along the West Street, Cloud 9 Café is a haven for those seeking refuge from the rain. The large windows offer a misty view of the karst peaks, creating an almost surreal atmosphere. Their signature ginger-infused hot chocolate is a must-try, perfectly balancing sweetness and spice. Pair it with their homemade banana bread, and you’ve got the ultimate rainy-day comfort combo.

2. The Alley Cat Coffee

Tucked away in a quieter part of town, The Alley Cat Coffee is a hidden gem. The rustic wooden interiors and soft jazz music make it an ideal spot to unwind. Their pumpkin spice latte is a seasonal favorite, while the avocado toast with poached eggs keeps brunch lovers coming back. Don’t forget to browse their collection of travel books while you sip your coffee.

Hearty Local Cuisine to Warm You Up

3. Xiao Ma’s Noodle House

Rainy days call for steaming bowls of noodles, and Xiao Ma’s delivers just that. This family-run spot is famous for its beef rice noodles (mifen), slow-cooked in a rich broth with tender slices of meat. The spicy version is particularly popular, guaranteed to chase away the chill. The no-frills, homey vibe adds to the authenticity of the experience.

4. Moon Hill Hot Pot

If you’re craving something communal and warming, Moon Hill Hot Pot is the place to be. Their tomato-based broth is a local specialty, packed with umami and perfect for dipping fresh vegetables, tofu, and thinly sliced pork. The restaurant’s open kitchen lets you watch the chefs at work, adding to the lively atmosphere.

International Flavors for a Global Escape

5. The Bavarian Beerhouse

For those missing European comfort food, The Bavarian Beerhouse offers a hearty escape. Their sausage platter with sauerkraut and mustard is a crowd-pleaser, while the warm pretzels with beer cheese dip are pure indulgence. The wooden benches and dim lighting create a snug, tavern-like feel—ideal for a rainy afternoon.

6. Pizza Alley

Sometimes, all you want on a gloomy day is a cheesy, crispy pizza. Pizza Alley serves up wood-fired pies with a local twist—try the Li River Special, topped with local mushrooms and smoked bacon. The open kitchen lets you watch the flames dance as your pizza bakes, adding a bit of drama to your meal.

Upscale Dining with a Side of Ambiance

7. The Giggling Tree

A short bike ride from town, The Giggling Tree is worth the trip, especially on a drizzly day. This boutique guesthouse and restaurant offers a refined take on local dishes. Their slow-cooked duck with star anise is a masterpiece, best enjoyed with a glass of local rice wine. The glass-walled dining room ensures you don’t miss the misty mountain views.

8. Riverside Retreat

For a truly luxurious experience, Riverside Retreat combines fine dining with breathtaking river views. Their truffle-infused wild mushroom risotto is a standout, rich and creamy—the kind of dish that makes you forget the weather outside. The elegant décor and attentive service make it perfect for a romantic rainy evening.

Sweet Treats to Brighten a Gray Day

9. Sugar & Spice Bakery

No rainy day is complete without dessert, and Sugar & Spice Bakery has you covered. Their warm cinnamon rolls are legendary, drizzled with caramel and served fresh from the oven. If you’re feeling adventurous, try the durian cheesecake—a bold fusion of East and West.

10. The Chocolate Factory

Yes, Yangshuo has its own chocolate haven. The Chocolate Factory crafts artisanal treats, from dark chocolate truffles to white chocolate matcha bars. Their hot chocolate flight lets you sample three different varieties—perfect for sharing (or not). The cozy seating nooks make it easy to linger.

Final Thoughts

Rain in Yangshuo doesn’t have to put a damper on your travels. Whether you’re craving local flavors, international dishes, or just a great cup of coffee, these spots turn a wet day into a culinary adventure. So grab a seat, savor the moment, and let the rain outside become part of the magic.
