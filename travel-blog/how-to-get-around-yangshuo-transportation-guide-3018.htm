How to Get Around Yangshuo: Transportation Guide

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Navigating the stunning karst landscape of Yangshuo is an adventure in itself. This guide will walk you through every possible way to traverse this beautiful region, from bustling streets to serene riverways. Whether you're an adrenaline junkie or a leisure seeker, getting around Yangshuo is part of the unforgettable experience.

Arriving in Yangshuo: Gateways to Paradise

Most travelers begin their journey by arriving at a major transportation hub before making their way to Yangshuo.

By Air: Flying into Guilin Liangjiang International Airport (KWL)

The closest major airport is in Guilin, about 90 minutes to two hours from Yangshuo. Upon landing, you have several options: - Airport Shuttle Bus: Direct shuttles to Yangshuo are available, though schedules can be limited. It's affordable but might not align with your flight timing. - Taxi or Pre-booked Car: This is the most convenient but expensive option. Negotiate the fare beforehand or use a ride-hailing app to avoid overcharging. - Public Bus to Guilin City First: Take a bus to Guilin city center (around 45 minutes), then transfer to a Yangshuo-bound bus. This is cheaper but time-consuming.

By High-Speed Train: Arriving at Guilin Railway Stations

Guilin has three main train stations: Guilin, Guilin North, and Guilin West. High-speed trains connect Guilin to major cities like Beijing, Shanghai, and Guangzhou. From any station: - Direct Bus to Yangshuo: Buses run frequently from Guilin Bus Station to Yangshuo, taking about 1.5 hours. - Taxi or DiDi: More flexible but costlier. Ideal for groups or those with heavy luggage.

By Long-Distance Bus

If you're coming from nearby cities like Nanning or Liuzhou, long-distance buses are a reliable option. They arrive at Yangshuo Bus Station, which is centrally located and easy to navigate from.

Getting Around Yangshuo Town: The Heart of the Action

Yangshuo town is compact but bustling. Here’s how to explore it like a pro.

On Foot: The Best Way to Soak In the Vibes

The town center is very walkable. Stroll along West Street (Xi Jie), lined with shops, cafes, and bars. Walking allows you to discover hidden gems, like local tea houses or street art, at your own pace.

Bicycle Rentals: Embrace the Local Vibe

Renting a bike is cheap, eco-friendly, and incredibly popular. You can find rentals everywhere for as low as 20-50 RMB per day. - Routes: Cycle along the Yulong River for breathtaking views of rice paddies and karst peaks. The Ten-Mile Gallery route is a must-do, passing through picturesque villages and farmland. - Tips: Opt for a mountain bike if you plan to venture off-road. Always test the brakes and tires before heading out.

Electric Scooters: For the Adventurous Souls

E-scooters offer more speed and range than bicycles. They’re perfect for covering longer distances, like riding to Xianggong Mountain for sunrise. - Rental Spots: Numerous shops in town rent them by the hour or day. Expect to pay around 50-100 RMB depending on the model. - Safety First: Wear a helmet (though it’s often overlooked by locals) and be cautious on busy roads. Police sometimes check for licenses, so stick to less trafficked areas.

Taxis and DiDi: Quick and Convenient

Taxis are metered but rare in peak seasons. DiDi (China’s Uber) is more reliable. Use it for short trips within town or to nearby attractions like the Liu Sanjie Light Show.

Local Buses: Budget-Friendly but Challenging

Local buses connect Yangshuo to surrounding villages. They’re cheap (5-10 RMB) but routes can be confusing for non-Mandarin speakers. Have your destination written in Chinese to show the driver.

Exploring the Countryside: Beyond Yangshuo Town

The real magic of Yangshuo lies in its countryside. Here’s how to access those iconic spots.

Motorbike Taxis: For Short Hops

Common in rural areas, these are cheap and thrilling. Negotiate the fare before hopping on. Great for reaching trailheads or remote villages.

Private Car Hire: Comfort and Flexibility

Hire a car with a driver for a half or full day (around 300-600 RMB). This is ideal for families or groups wanting to visit multiple spots like Moon Hill, Silver Cave, or Dragon Bridge without hassle.

Organized Tours: Hassle-Free Exploration

Many hotels and agencies offer day tours via minivan or coach. These often include transportation, entrance fees, and a guide. Perfect for those who prefer a structured itinerary.

River Transportation: The Scenic Route

Yangshuo’s rivers are lifelines. Experiencing them is non-negotiable.

Bamboo Rafting: A Must-Do Experience

Bamboo rafts on the Yulong or Li River are iconic. Prices vary by route (e.g., 100-200 RMB per person for a 1-2 hour ride). - Yulong River: Calmer and less crowded, perfect for a peaceful float. - Li River: Offers dramatic views but can be touristy. Book through reputable providers to avoid scams.

Boats and Cruises: For a Grand Perspective

From Xingping, you can take short boat trips to see the Li River’s highlights, like the spot on the 20 RMB note. Longer cruises from Guilin to Yangshuo are also available, though they take half a day.

Day Trips from Yangshuo: Expanding Your Horizons

Yangshuo is a great base for exploring Guangxi’s wonders.

To Longji Rice Terraces: A Breathtaking Journey

The terraces are 2-3 hours away. Options include: - Direct Bus: Buses from Yangshuo to Longsheng are available but require a transfer to the terraces. - Private Transfer: More convenient, especially if you’re short on time. Costs around 600-800 RMB round trip.

To Xingping Ancient Town: Stepping Back in Time

Xingping is 45 minutes by bus. Buses leave frequently from Yangshuo Bus Station. Alternatively, cycle along the river path for a memorable journey.

Practical Tips for Navigating Yangshuo

  • Cash is King: While Alipay/WeChat Pay are common, rural drivers and rentals might prefer cash.
  • Bargain Politely: Negotiate for rentals and taxis, but don’t overdo it. A smile goes a long way.
  • Avoid Peak Seasons: During Chinese holidays, transportation gets overcrowded. Book in advance if possible.
  • Use Maps.me or Baidu Maps: Google Maps is unreliable in China. Offline maps can be a lifesaver.
  • Learn Basic Mandarin Phrases: Knowing how to say "how much" (duo shao qian) or "where is" (zai na li) can help immensely.

Yangshuo’s transportation options are as diverse as its landscape. Whether you’re cycling through emerald fields or rafting down tranquil rivers, each mode of travel adds a unique layer to your adventure.
