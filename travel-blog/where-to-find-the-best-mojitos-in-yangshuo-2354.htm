Where to Find the Best Mojitos in Yangshuo

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Yangshuo, nestled in the heart of Guangxi’s karst mountains, is a traveler’s paradise. Known for its breathtaking landscapes, adventurous outdoor activities, and vibrant nightlife, this small town has become a must-visit destination for backpackers and luxury travelers alike. But beyond the Li River cruises and moonlit bamboo rafting, there’s another reason to fall in love with Yangshuo—its thriving bar scene, particularly its refreshing mojitos.

Whether you’re winding down after a day of rock climbing or simply soaking in the bohemian vibes of West Street, a well-crafted mojito can be the perfect companion. Here’s your ultimate guide to finding the best mojitos in Yangshuo.

The Mojito Magic: Why Yangshuo Does It Right

Before diving into the best spots, let’s talk about why Yangshuo’s mojitos stand out. The town’s tropical climate, fresh local ingredients (think hand-picked mint and sugarcane), and the influence of global backpacker culture have created a unique cocktail scene. Bartenders here often put their own spin on the classic recipe—some infuse it with lychee or passionfruit, while others stick to the traditional Cuban style with a splash of Yangshuo flair.

Top Spots for the Best Mojitos in Yangshuo

1. Mojito Lounge on West Street

Location: Heart of West Street

West Street is the epicenter of Yangshuo’s nightlife, and Mojito Lounge is its crown jewel. This open-air bar is famous for its laid-back ambiance and expertly mixed drinks. Their signature Karst Mojito adds a hint of local honey, giving it a smooth, slightly sweet finish. Pair it with their crispy spring rolls, and you’ve got the perfect start to your evening.

Pro Tip: Arrive before 8 PM to snag a rooftop seat with a view of the illuminated karst peaks.

2. The Hidden Garden Bar

Location: A quiet alley off West Street

Tucked away from the hustle and bustle, The Hidden Garden Bar is a favorite among expats and long-term travelers. Their Ginger Mojito is a game-changer—fresh ginger muddled with mint and lime creates a spicy kick that’s oddly addictive. The bar’s rustic charm, complete with fairy lights and bamboo decor, makes it an ideal spot for a relaxed night out.

Must-Try: Ask for their "Mojito Flight," a sampler of three different flavors.

3. Riverside Cocktail Club

Location: Along the Li River

For those who prefer their cocktails with a side of serenity, Riverside Cocktail Club is the place to be. Overlooking the water, this spot serves a Lychee Mojito that’s become legendary among visitors. The sweetness of lychee balances the tartness of lime, making it dangerously easy to sip.

Bonus: They often host live acoustic music at sunset—timing your visit for golden hour is highly recommended.

4. Moonlight Bar & Grill

Location: Near Yangshuo Park

Moonlight Bar & Grill is where locals and tourists mingle over hearty food and strong drinks. Their Spicy Basil Mojito is a bold twist on the classic, using Thai basil and a dash of chili for an unexpected punch. If you’re feeling adventurous, this is the mojito to try.

Insider Knowledge: Their happy hour (5–7 PM) includes buy-one-get-one-free mojitos.

5. The Drifter’s Den

Location: Just outside the main tourist zone

A bit off the beaten path, The Drifter’s Den caters to the free-spirited crowd. Their Passionfruit Mojito is a tropical dream, blending tart passionfruit pulp with the usual minty freshness. The bar’s reggae playlist and hammock seating add to the carefree vibe.

Don’t Miss: Their "Mojito Mondays" promo—half-price mojitos all night.

How to Spot a Great Mojito in Yangshuo

Not all mojitos are created equal. Here’s what to look for when judging your drink:

Fresh Ingredients

The best bars use hand-crushed mint, freshly squeezed lime juice, and real sugarcane syrup (not the pre-made sugary stuff).

The Right Balance

A perfect mojito should be neither too sweet nor too sour. The rum should be present but not overpowering.

Presentation

Look for a glass packed with ice, vibrant mint leaves, and a lime wedge garnish. If it’s served in a plastic cup with a straw, you might want to reconsider.

Beyond the Mojito: Other Must-Try Yangshuo Drinks

While mojitos are the star, Yangshuo’s bar scene offers other gems:

  • Guilin Mule – A local take on the Moscow Mule, using Guilin rice wine instead of vodka.
  • Bamboo Punch – A fruity, rum-based cocktail served in a bamboo cup.
  • Osmanthus Sour – A floral whiskey sour made with osmanthus syrup, a regional specialty.

Final Sips

Yangshuo’s mojito scene is as diverse as its landscape—whether you prefer yours classic, fruity, or with a spicy twist, there’s a bar in town that’s perfected it. So next time you’re biking through rice paddies or exploring hidden caves, save some energy for a mojito-fueled night out. Cheers to unforgettable sips under the karst mountain sky!
