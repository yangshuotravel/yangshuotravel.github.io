Yangshuo’s Fishing Villages: When to Explore

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Nestled along the serene Li River, Yangshuo’s fishing villages are a hidden gem in Guangxi’s karst landscape. These timeless settlements offer a glimpse into rural Chinese life, where bamboo rafts glide across emerald waters and mist-clad peaks frame every horizon. But when is the best time to explore these picturesque villages? Let’s dive into the seasonal rhythms, local festivals, and insider tips to help you plan the perfect trip.

The Best Seasons to Visit Yangshuo’s Fishing Villages

Spring: Blossoms and Mild Weather (March–May)

Spring transforms Yangshuo into a watercolor painting. The temperatures hover between 15°C and 25°C (59°F–77°F), making it ideal for leisurely village walks or cycling through terraced fields. Key highlights:
- Peach and rapeseed blooms: The countryside bursts with pink and yellow hues, especially around Xingping and Fuli.
- Fewer crowds: Avoid the Golden Week rush in early May for a quieter experience.
- Cormorant fishing displays: Local fishermen showcase this ancient tradition as the river awakens.

Pro tip: Pack a light rain jacket—spring showers are common but fleeting.

Summer: Lush Greenery and Vibrant Energy (June–August)

While temperatures soar above 30°C (86°F), summer has its own magic:
- Li River’s emerald waters: Perfect for bamboo rafting or kayaking near villages like Huangyao.
- Dragon Boat Festival: Join locals in June for races and zongzi (sticky rice dumplings).
- Night markets: Sample grilled fish and local beers in Yangshuo’s bustling West Street.

Warning: Midday heat can be intense. Explore villages early morning or late afternoon.

Autumn: Golden Landscapes and Harvest Vibes (September–November)

Autumn is the crowd favorite—cool air (20°C–28°C / 68°F–82°F) and clear skies enhance the views:
- Harvest season: Watch farmers drying rice and chili peppers in villages like Dutou.
- Photographer’s paradise: Capture the golden light on karst peaks at sunrise.
- Moon Hill hikes: Combine village visits with scenic treks.

Insider move: Visit in late September to avoid National Day crowds (October 1–7).

Winter: Tranquility and Misty Mystique (December–February)

Winter brings fewer tourists and a dreamy atmosphere:
- Misty river scenes: The Li River shrouded in fog feels like an ink-wash painting.
- Lower prices: Hotels and tours offer discounts.
- Local New Year festivities: Experience pre-Chunyan celebrations with lanterns and folk dances.

Note: Temperatures drop to 5°C–15°C (41°F–59°F). Layer up!

Hidden Fishing Villages You Can’t Miss

Xingping: The Postcard-Perfect Classic

Famous for gracing the 20 RMB note, Xingping blends tourism and tradition:
- Sunrise at Laozhai Mountain: Hike for a panoramic village-and-river view.
- Old Street: Cobbled lanes lined with Ming-era homes and fish-themed souvenirs.

Fuli: The Umbrella-Making Hub

Beyond fishing, Fuli is known for hand-painted oil-paper umbrellas:
- Umbrella workshops: Try crafting your own (great for rainy spring visits).
- Riverside BBQ: Freshly caught fish grilled with local spices.

Huangyao: The Time-Capsule Town

A 2-hour drive from Yangshuo, this 1,000-year-old village is a detour worth taking:
- Ancient banyan trees: Framing stone bridges and ancestral halls.
- Mid-April festival: Shadow puppetry and tea-picking dances.

Local Experiences to Elevate Your Trip

Learn from the Fishermen

  • Cormorant fishing demos: Book a sunset tour to see birds dive for fish (best in spring/autumn).
  • Bamboo raft DIY: Some villages let you build—and float—your own mini raft.

Taste the River’s Bounty

  • Beer Fish: Yangshuo’s signature dish, made with Li River carp and local beer.
  • Snail Noodles: Try this Guilin specialty in family-run village eateries.

Capture the Culture

  • Rice terrace homestays: Sleep in a farmer’s guesthouse in Longji (nearby).
  • Fishing net workshops: Weave nets with villagers in quieter spots like Caoping.

Practical Tips for Your Visit

  • Transport: Rent an e-bike to explore villages at your pace.
  • Respect: Avoid intrusive photography of locals without permission.
  • Cash: Smaller villages may not accept digital payments.

Yangshuo’s fishing villages are more than a backdrop—they’re a living tapestry of culture, nature, and history. Whether you chase spring blossoms or winter mist, each season unveils a new side of this riverside wonderland.
