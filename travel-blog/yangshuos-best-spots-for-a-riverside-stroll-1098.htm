Yangshuo’s Best Spots for a Riverside Stroll

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Yangshuo, nestled in the heart of Guangxi’s karst landscape, is a dream destination for travelers seeking serenity and adventure. While its towering limestone peaks and vibrant nightlife often steal the spotlight, the riverside walks here offer an equally enchanting experience. Whether you’re a solo wanderer, a couple seeking romance, or a family looking for a leisurely outing, these riverside spots promise unforgettable moments.

The Iconic Li River Promenade

Why It’s a Must-Visit

The Li River is the lifeline of Yangshuo, and its promenade is the perfect place to soak in the region’s natural beauty. Stretching along the west bank of the river, this walkway offers unobstructed views of the karst mountains reflected in the shimmering water. Early mornings are magical here, with mist clinging to the peaks and fishermen gliding by on bamboo rafts.

Best Time to Go

Sunrise and sunset are the golden hours. The soft light paints the landscape in hues of pink and gold, making it a photographer’s paradise. Evenings are lively, with street performers and local vendors adding to the charm.

Xingping’s Riverside Charm

A Step Back in Time

Xingping, a quaint village about an hour from Yangshuo, is where the Li River’s beauty reaches its peak. The riverside path here is quieter, lined with old banyan trees and traditional fishing boats. This is the spot immortalized on the 20 RMB note, and the view is just as breathtaking in person.

Hidden Gems Along the Way

  • Lao Zhai Shan: A short detour leads to this hill, offering a panoramic view of the river snaking through the karst formations.
  • Fisherman’s Wharf: Watch local fishermen demonstrate their centuries-old cormorant fishing techniques—a dying art that’s fascinating to witness.

The Yulong River: Serenity Unleashed

Bamboo Rafts and Riverside Trails

Less commercialized than the Li River, the Yulong River is ideal for those seeking peace. The riverside path here is shaded by bamboo groves, and the occasional bamboo raft floats by, carrying travelers enjoying a slower pace.

Must-Stop Spots

  • Jiuxian Village: A picturesque farming village where you can detour for a cup of tea at a riverside café.
  • Dragon Bridge: An ancient stone bridge that’s perfect for a mid-walk break. The surrounding rice paddies add to the rural charm.

Moon Hill’s Riverside Path

Combine Hiking and Strolling

Moon Hill is famous for its crescent-shaped hole, but the riverside path leading to it is equally rewarding. The trail follows a gentle stream, with opportunities to dip your feet in the water or picnic on the rocks.

Pro Tip

Visit in the late afternoon when the sun casts a glow through Moon Hill’s arch. The reflection on the water is surreal.

Fuli Town’s Artistic Flair

Where Culture Meets Nature

Fuli, known for its hand-painted fans, has a riverside walk that blends art and nature. The path is dotted with workshops where you can watch artisans at work. The river here is calm, perfect for a reflective stroll.

Don’t Miss

  • Fuli Market: Held every few days, this market is a great place to pick up souvenirs and sample local snacks.

Practical Tips for Your Riverside Adventure

What to Bring

  • Comfortable walking shoes (some paths are uneven).
  • A reusable water bottle—stay hydrated!
  • Sunscreen and a hat, especially in summer.

Getting Around

Biking is a popular way to explore these riverside paths. Rentals are cheap, and the flat terrain makes it easy for all skill levels.

Respect the Environment

Yangshuo’s beauty is fragile. Stick to marked paths, avoid littering, and support local businesses to help preserve this paradise.

Whether you’re chasing sunrise hues over the Li River or wandering the quiet trails of the Yulong, Yangshuo’s riverside strolls are a gateway to the soul of this enchanting region.
