Cycling to Xingping from Yangshuo: Rental Bike Guide

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Yangshuo and Xingping are two of the most picturesque destinations in Guangxi, China, known for their stunning karst landscapes, serene rivers, and charming rural vibes. One of the best ways to explore the region is by cycling from Yangshuo to Xingping. This route offers breathtaking views, cultural encounters, and a chance to experience the countryside at your own pace. Whether you're an avid cyclist or a casual rider, this guide will help you navigate bike rentals, routes, and must-see stops along the way.

Why Cycle from Yangshuo to Xingping?

Cycling is the perfect way to soak in the beauty of the Li River and its surrounding karst mountains. Unlike buses or taxis, biking allows you to stop whenever you want—whether it's for a photo op, a quick snack, or a chat with local farmers. The 25-kilometer ride is manageable for most fitness levels, and the flat terrain makes it enjoyable rather than exhausting.

Scenic Highlights Along the Route

  • Ten-Mile Gallery (Shili Gallery): A stretch of road flanked by towering limestone peaks and rice paddies.
  • Yulong River: A quieter alternative to the Li River, perfect for a detour or a bamboo raft ride.
  • Ancient Banyan Tree: A 1,400-year-old tree near Yangshuo, often featured in Chinese paintings.
  • Xingping Old Town: A well-preserved ancient town with cobblestone streets and Ming-Qing dynasty architecture.

Where to Rent a Bike in Yangshuo

Yangshuo is packed with bike rental shops, ranging from budget options to high-end mountain bikes. Here’s what you need to know before renting:

Types of Bikes Available

  1. Standard Bikes (20-40 RMB/day): Basic single-speed or multi-speed bicycles, ideal for casual riders.
  2. Mountain Bikes (50-100 RMB/day): Sturdier with better suspension, great for off-road detours.
  3. Electric Bikes (80-150 RMB/day): A battery-powered option if you want to save energy.
  4. Tandem Bikes (60-100 RMB/day): Fun for couples or friends riding together.

Top Rental Shops in Yangshuo

  • Yangshuo Bike Rental (West Street): Conveniently located, offers well-maintained bikes.
  • Giggling Tree Bike Shop: Known for friendly service and quality mountain bikes.
  • Bamboo Bike Rentals: Eco-friendly options with bamboo-framed bikes.

Rental Tips

  • Always test the brakes and gears before leaving the shop.
  • Ask for a lock and a repair kit (some shops include them for free).
  • Negotiate for multi-day rentals—discounts are often available.

The Best Cycling Route: Yangshuo to Xingping

The most scenic route follows the Yulong River before merging with the Li River. Here’s a step-by-step breakdown:

Stage 1: Yangshuo to Jiuxian Village (10 km)

Start early to avoid midday heat. Head north from Yangshuo toward Jiuxian Village, passing through rice fields and small villages. Stop at Butterfly Spring Park if you want a short detour.

Stage 2: Jiuxian Village to Xingping (15 km)

From Jiuxian, follow signs to Xingping. The road runs parallel to the Li River, offering postcard-worthy views. Key stops:

  • Nine Horses Fresco Hill: A famous karst formation said to resemble nine horses.
  • 20 RMB Note Scenic Spot: The iconic view featured on China’s 20 RMB bill.

Alternative Route: Yulong River Detour

For a quieter ride, take the Yulong River path. You’ll pass bamboo groves, water buffalo, and small bridges. This adds about 5 km but is worth it for the tranquility.

What to Pack for the Ride

  • Water and Snacks: Hydration is key—bring at least 1L of water.
  • Sunscreen and Hat: The sun can be intense, even in cooler months.
  • Phone with Maps: Offline maps (like Maps.me) help if you lose signal.
  • Cash (RMB): Small villages may not accept digital payments.

Safety Tips for Cycling in Guangxi

  • Stay on Designated Paths: Some roads are shared with scooters and trucks.
  • Wear a Helmet: Many rental shops provide them—use one!
  • Watch for Weather: Rain can make roads slippery; check forecasts before setting out.

Exploring Xingping After the Ride

Once you arrive, park your bike and explore:

  • Xingping Fishing Village: A glimpse into traditional riverside life.
  • Laozhai Mountain Hike: A short but steep climb for panoramic views.
  • Local Eats: Try Guilin rice noodles or beer fish (píjiǔ yú).

Returning Your Bike

Most rental shops allow you to drop off bikes in Xingping for a small fee (around 30-50 RMB). Alternatively, cycle back to Yangshuo if you’re up for the round trip.

Cycling from Yangshuo to Xingping is more than just a ride—it’s an immersive journey through one of China’s most beautiful landscapes. With the right bike and a sense of adventure, you’ll create memories that last far beyond the trip.
