Spring Kayaking on the Yulong River

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

The Yulong River, a lesser-known but equally breathtaking counterpart to the Li River, offers one of the most tranquil and picturesque kayaking experiences in China’s Guangxi region. As spring breathes life into the karst mountains and emerald waters, there’s no better time to paddle through this serene landscape. Whether you’re an adventure seeker or a nature lover, kayaking on the Yulong River is an unforgettable way to immerse yourself in Yangshuo’s natural beauty.

Why Kayak the Yulong River in Spring?

Spring (March to May) is arguably the best season to explore the Yulong River. The weather is mild, with temperatures ranging from 15°C to 25°C (59°F to 77°F), making it perfect for outdoor activities. The riverbanks burst into life with blooming flowers, and the surrounding karst peaks are shrouded in a light mist, creating a dreamy, almost surreal atmosphere.

Fewer Crowds, More Serenity

Unlike the bustling Li River, the Yulong River remains relatively untouched by mass tourism. Spring, in particular, sees fewer visitors compared to peak summer months, allowing for a more peaceful and intimate experience.

Ideal Water Levels

After the winter rains, the river’s water levels are just right—neither too shallow nor too rapid. This makes kayaking smoother and safer, especially for beginners.

The Kayaking Experience: What to Expect

Starting Points and Routes

Most kayaking tours begin near the Yulong Bridge, a scenic spot about 20 minutes from Yangshuo by bike or taxi. From here, you can choose between two primary routes:

  1. The Short Route (1-2 hours): Perfect for beginners or those short on time, this route takes you past small rapids, bamboo groves, and traditional waterwheels.
  2. The Long Route (3-4 hours): For a more immersive experience, this route extends to Gongnong Bridge, passing hidden villages, limestone caves, and secluded swimming spots.

Highlights Along the Way

Bamboo Forests and Waterwheels

The riverbanks are lined with towering bamboo groves, their reflections shimmering in the calm waters. You’ll also encounter ancient waterwheels, some still in use by local farmers—a glimpse into rural Chinese life.

Hidden Swimming Holes

On warmer days, take a break from paddling and dive into one of the river’s crystal-clear swimming holes. Locals often frequent these spots, and joining them for a refreshing dip is a great way to connect with the community.

Karst Cave Explorations

Some tours include stops at small, accessible caves along the river. These hidden gems are adorned with stalactites and offer a cool respite from the sun.

Tips for a Perfect Kayaking Trip

What to Bring

  • Sun Protection: A hat, sunglasses, and sunscreen are must-haves, as the sun can be intense even in spring.
  • Waterproof Bag: Keep your phone, camera, and snacks dry.
  • Comfortable Clothing: Quick-dry clothes and water shoes are ideal.
  • Snacks and Water: While some tours provide refreshments, it’s always good to have your own.

Choosing a Tour Operator

Opt for a reputable local operator with English-speaking guides. Many offer combo packages that include biking to the river or a post-kayking countryside lunch.

Safety First

While the Yulong River is generally calm, always wear a life jacket and listen to your guide’s instructions, especially if navigating minor rapids.

Beyond Kayaking: Other Spring Activities in Yangshuo

Cycling the Countryside

Rent a bike and explore the flat, scenic paths along the river. Stop at local farms or tea houses for a taste of rural Guangxi.

Rock Climbing

Yangshuo is a global rock-climbing hub, and spring’s cool temperatures make it an ideal time to scale the karst cliffs.

Cormorant Fishing Shows

At dusk, witness the ancient practice of cormorant fishing on the Li River—a cultural experience that pairs perfectly with a day of kayaking.

Final Thoughts

Kayaking the Yulong River in spring is more than just an activity—it’s a journey through a living postcard. The combination of stunning scenery, cultural encounters, and the gentle rhythm of paddling creates a travel memory that lingers long after the trip ends. Whether you’re gliding past water buffalo or drifting under arched stone bridges, every moment on the Yulong River feels like a step into a quieter, more magical world.
