How to Enjoy Yangshuo’s Rivers Without Polluting

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Yangshuo’s rivers are the lifeblood of its breathtaking landscape. The Li River, Yulong River, and their tributaries weave through karst mountains, creating a scene so iconic it’s printed on China’s currency. But as tourism booms, so does the risk of pollution. Here’s how to savor these waterways responsibly—keeping them pristine for future travelers.

Why Yangshuo’s Rivers Matter

A Natural Masterpiece

The rivers here aren’t just pretty backdrops. They sustain local ecosystems, support agriculture, and define the region’s culture. Fish species like the Yangshuo catfish thrive in these waters, while farmers rely on them for rice paddies. Polluting them disrupts this delicate balance.

Tourism’s Double-Edged Sword

Bamboo rafting, kayaking, and swimming draw millions yearly. But discarded plastic bottles, sunscreen chemicals, and motorboat fuel threaten water quality. In 2019, cleanup crews removed over 2 tons of trash from the Yulong River alone.

How to Enjoy the Rivers Sustainably

Choose Eco-Friendly Activities

Bamboo Rafting Done Right

Opt for rafts manned by local operators using traditional poles—not motorized ones. Companies like Yangshuo Eco-Rafts prioritize low-impact tours, even providing reusable bamboo cups instead of plastic.

Swim Smart

Avoid sunscreens with oxybenzone, which harms aquatic life. Mineral-based options (zinc oxide) are safer. Stick to designated swimming areas to prevent erosion of riverbanks.

Pack It In, Pack It Out

Zero-Waste Essentials

  • Reusable water bottles: Refill stations are available in Xingping and Yangshuo town.
  • Biodegradable soap: For camping or washing hands.
  • Trash bags: Even fruit peels take weeks to decompose.

Pro tip: Join a “plogging” (jogging + picking up litter) group—Yangshuo’s hiking trails often organize these.

Support Conservation Efforts

Volunteer with Local NGOs

Groups like Guardians of the Li River host monthly cleanups. A 2-hour effort can remove hundreds of cigarette butts and food wrappers.

Dine at River-Friendly Cafés

Restaurants like Riverside Kitchen use compostable packaging and donate profits to waterway protection.

What Not to Do

Avoid Motorized Tours

Speedboats churn up sediment and leak fuel. If you must, choose electric models (e.g., Li River E-Cruises).

Never Release Lanterns or Balloons

Sky lanterns often land in rivers, harming wildlife. Try floating flower offerings instead—a local tradition that’s biodegradable.

Skip the Disposable Rain Gear

Plastic ponchos tear easily, becoming river trash. Pack a lightweight, reusable rain jacket.

The Bigger Picture: Yangshuo’s Future

Hotels and tour operators are increasingly adopting green certifications. Look for the Green Yangshuo badge when booking. Even small choices—like refusing a plastic straw—add up.

By traveling mindfully, you’re not just a visitor. You’re part of the solution. The rivers will thank you with clearer waters, healthier fish, and uninterrupted views of those legendary karst peaks.
