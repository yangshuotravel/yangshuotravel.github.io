Sunrise & Sunset Spots in Yangshuo’s Countryside

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Yangshuo’s countryside is a dreamscape of karst mountains, winding rivers, and lush rice paddies. But what truly elevates this region to magical status are its breathtaking sunrises and sunsets. Whether you’re an early riser chasing the first light or a sunset chaser savoring golden hour, Yangshuo offers some of the most picturesque spots in all of China. Here’s your ultimate guide to the best sunrise and sunset viewpoints in Yangshuo’s countryside.

The Best Sunrise Spots in Yangshuo

1. Xianggong Mountain (Xiānggōng Shān)

If there’s one place every traveler should visit for sunrise, it’s Xianggong Mountain. This viewpoint offers an unobstructed panorama of the Li River snaking through the karst peaks. The hike to the top takes about 20-30 minutes, but the effort is rewarded with a surreal vista as the sun paints the sky in hues of pink and gold.

Pro Tip: Arrive at least 30 minutes before sunrise to secure a good spot—this location is popular among photographers.

2. Moon Hill (Yuèliàng Shān)

While Moon Hill is famous for its crescent-shaped natural arch, it’s also a fantastic spot for early morning light. The hike is moderately challenging, but once you reach the viewing platforms, you’ll witness the misty valleys awakening under the soft glow of dawn.

Bonus: If you’re up for an adventure, combine your sunrise hike with a visit to the nearby Big Banyan Tree afterward.

3. Yulong River (Yùlóng Hé) Bamboo Rafts

For a more serene sunrise experience, take a bamboo raft along the Yulong River at first light. The stillness of the water mirrors the surrounding peaks, creating a double landscape effect. Many local operators offer early-morning tours—just be sure to book in advance.

The Most Spectacular Sunset Locations

1. Laozhai Mountain (Lǎozhài Shān)

A lesser-known gem, Laozhai Mountain provides an incredible sunset view without the crowds. The hike is steep but short (about 15 minutes), and once at the top, you’ll see the sun dip behind the karst formations, casting long shadows over the countryside.

Insider Tip: Bring a picnic and enjoy the fading light in peace—this spot is perfect for solitude seekers.

2. Ten-Mile Gallery (Shílǐ Huáláng)

The Ten-Mile Gallery is a scenic cycling route by day, but as evening approaches, it transforms into a golden paradise. Rent a bike and pedal along the path as the setting sun bathes the rice fields and limestone peaks in warm light. Stop at one of the roadside cafes for a cold beer while you soak in the view.

3. West Street (Xī Jiē) Rooftop Bars

If you prefer a more relaxed sunset experience, head to one of Yangshuo’s rooftop bars along West Street. Sip on a cocktail as you watch the sky turn fiery over the distant mountains. It’s the perfect way to unwind after a day of exploring.

Hidden Gems for Sunrise & Sunset

1. Fuli Bridge (Fúlǐ Qiáo)

This ancient stone bridge is a quiet spot where locals often gather at dusk. The reflections of the bridge and surrounding hills in the water make for stunning sunset photography.

2. Jiu Xian Village (Jiǔxiān Cūn)

A small, traditional village tucked away from the tourist trail, Jiu Xian offers an authentic rural sunrise experience. Wake up early to see farmers tending to their fields as the first light touches the misty mountains.

Photography Tips for Capturing Yangshuo’s Light

  • Use a tripod for long exposures, especially during the blue hour before sunrise or after sunset.
  • Play with reflections—the rivers and rice paddies create mirror-like surfaces.
  • Arrive early for sunrise spots to avoid crowds and secure the best angles.

Final Thoughts on Timing & Logistics

  • Best Seasons: Spring (March-May) and autumn (September-November) offer the clearest skies.
  • Transport: Rent an e-bike or hire a local driver for early-morning trips to remote spots.
  • Local Guides: Consider hiring a guide for off-the-beaten-path locations—they know the best-hidden viewpoints.

Yangshuo’s countryside is a canvas of ever-changing light, and whether you’re chasing dawn or dusk, each moment here feels like a masterpiece. Pack your camera, set your alarm, and get ready for some of the most unforgettable sunrises and sunsets of your life.
