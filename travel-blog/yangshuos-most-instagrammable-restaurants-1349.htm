Yangshuo’s Most Instagrammable Restaurants

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Nestled among the breathtaking karst mountains and winding rivers of Guangxi, Yangshuo is a dream destination for travelers seeking both adventure and aesthetic charm. While the landscape alone is enough to fill your camera roll, the town’s vibrant culinary scene offers another layer of visual delight. From riverside bistros with panoramic views to hidden cafés adorned with fairy lights, Yangshuo’s restaurants are as photogenic as they are delicious. Here’s a curated list of the most Instagram-worthy spots to dine—and snap—in Yangshuo.

Riverside Dining with a View

1. The Giggling Tree

Perched on the banks of the Yulong River, The Giggling Tree is a European-style guesthouse and restaurant that feels like a secret garden. The outdoor terrace, draped in ivy and blooming flowers, overlooks emerald-green rice paddies and limestone peaks. Their wood-fired pizzas and homemade lemonade are as picture-perfect as the scenery. Pro tip: Visit at golden hour for a shot of your meal with the sun setting behind the mountains.

2. River View Restaurant

True to its name, River View Restaurant offers unobstructed vistas of the Li River from its open-air deck. The bamboo furnishings and rustic lanterns create a serene ambiance, while dishes like beer fish (a local specialty) arrive in colorful ceramic bowls. The reflection of the karst formations on the water makes for an unbeatable backdrop.

Charming Cafés & Brunch Spots

3. Luna Café

A favorite among digital nomads and coffee connoisseurs, Luna Café blends industrial chic with bohemian flair. Exposed brick walls, hanging plants, and latte art worthy of a close-up shot define this cozy spot. Their avocado toast and matcha pancakes are almost too pretty to eat—almost.

4. Mojo Café

Tucked away on a quiet alley, Mojo Café feels like a slice of Brooklyn in Yangshuo. The mismatched vintage furniture, shelves stacked with books, and chalkboard menus scream “Instagram bait.” Don’t leave without trying their rainbow bagels or turmeric lattes, both guaranteed to brighten your feed.

Hidden Gems with Unique Themes

5. The Alley Cat

This whimsical eatery is a cat lover’s paradise. With feline murals, paw-print coasters, and even resident kitties lounging around, The Alley Cat is as adorable as it sounds. Their heart-shaped waffles and cat-themed latte art are pure social media gold.

6. Zen Garden Tea House

For a tranquil escape, Zen Garden Tea House offers a minimalist aesthetic with bamboo sliding doors, stone pathways, and a koi pond. The traditional tea ceremony is a mesmerizing experience to capture on video, and their delicate dim sum plates are arranged like edible art.

Rooftop Bars & Sunset Spots

7. The Roof Top Bar at Yangshuo Mountain Retreat

Perched atop a boutique hotel, this bar boasts 360-degree views of the karst landscape. The infinity pool (yes, there’s a pool!) reflects the sky, creating a surreal effect. Sip on a lychee mojito while snapping a panoramic shot—#nofilter needed.

8. Cloud 9 Rooftop Lounge

A newcomer to Yangshuo’s scene, Cloud 9 lives up to its name with floating daybeds and fairy lights strung overhead. Their signature cocktails, like the “Karst Sunset” (a gradient-hued drink), are practically designed for Instagram Stories.

Local Eateries with Photogenic Flair

9. Xanadu Vegetarian Restaurant

Even meat-lovers will be dazzled by Xanadu’s creative plant-based dishes. Think dragon fruit smoothie bowls, lotus root salads, and tofu sculpted into intricate shapes. The restaurant’s bamboo décor and hand-painted menus add to its earthy charm.

10. West Street Noodle House

No trip to Yangshuo is complete without slurping down Guilin rice noodles. West Street Noodle House serves theirs in vibrant blue-and-white porcelain bowls, with toppings arranged like a colorful mosaic. The bustling open kitchen makes for great action shots.

Whether you’re a foodie, a photographer, or just someone who loves a good view, Yangshuo’s dining scene promises unforgettable visuals at every turn. So charge your camera, bring your appetite, and get ready to feast—both on the cuisine and the ‘gram-worthy moments.
