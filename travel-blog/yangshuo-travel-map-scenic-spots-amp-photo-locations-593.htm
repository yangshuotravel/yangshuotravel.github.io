Yangshuo Travel Map: Scenic Spots & Photo Locations

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Nestled in the heart of Guangxi Province, Yangshuo is a dream destination for travelers seeking breathtaking karst landscapes, serene rivers, and vibrant cultural experiences. Whether you're an adventure seeker, a photography enthusiast, or simply looking to unwind, this guide will help you navigate the best scenic spots and photo locations in Yangshuo.

The Must-Visit Scenic Spots

1. The Li River

No trip to Yangshuo is complete without a cruise or bamboo raft ride along the Li River. The iconic karst peaks reflected in the emerald-green waters create a scene straight out of a traditional Chinese painting.

Best Photo Spots:
- Xingping Town: The backdrop of the 20 RMB note features a stunning view of the river and peaks.
- Nine Horses Fresco Hill: A legendary cliff face said to resemble nine galloping horses.

2. Moon Hill

This natural limestone arch is one of Yangshuo’s most recognizable landmarks. A short but steep hike rewards visitors with panoramic views of the surrounding countryside.

Pro Tip: Visit during sunrise or sunset for the most dramatic lighting.

3. Yulong River

For a quieter alternative to the Li River, the Yulong River offers peaceful bamboo rafting and cycling routes. The ancient stone bridges and lush rice paddies make it a photographer’s paradise.

Don’t Miss:
- Jiuxian Ancient Village: A well-preserved Ming Dynasty village perfect for cultural snapshots.

Hidden Gems for Unique Photos

1. Butterfly Spring Park

This lesser-known park features a crystal-clear spring, a butterfly garden, and a hidden cave. The reflections of the karst peaks in the water create surreal compositions.

2. Silver Cave

A dazzling underground world of stalactites and stalagmites illuminated by colorful lights. The cave’s otherworldly formations are perfect for moody, dramatic shots.

3. Fuli Ancient Town

Known as the "Hometown of Chinese Fans," Fuli is a charming riverside town where artisans still hand-paint traditional paper fans. The narrow alleys and old wooden houses offer endless photo opportunities.

Adventure & Photography Hotspots

1. Bamboo Rafting on the Yulong River

Glide past water buffaloes and farmers tending rice fields while capturing the slow-paced rural life. Early morning fog adds a mystical touch to your shots.

2. Cycling Through the Countryside

Rent a bike and explore the winding paths between Yangshuo’s villages. The Ten-Mile Gallery route is particularly scenic, with karst peaks towering over golden rapeseed fields (best in spring).

3. Rock Climbing at Swiss Cheese Rock

Yangshuo is a world-class rock climbing destination. Even if you’re not climbing, the views from the base of the cliffs are spectacular.

Seasonal Highlights

Spring (March-May)

  • Rapeseed flowers blanket the fields in bright yellow.
  • The Li River is at its clearest after winter rains.

Summer (June-August)

  • Lush greenery and vibrant sunsets.
  • Early mornings are best to avoid crowds and heat.

Autumn (September-November)

  • Golden rice terraces near Longji are a short trip away.
  • Cooler temperatures make hiking more comfortable.

Winter (December-February)

  • Misty mornings create a dreamy, ethereal atmosphere.
  • Fewer tourists mean more peaceful explorations.

Practical Tips for Travelers

  • Best Time for Photos: Sunrise (5-7 AM) and sunset (5-7 PM) offer the softest light.
  • Drones: Check local regulations—some areas restrict drone use.
  • Local Guides: Hiring a guide can lead you to secret viewpoints.

Yangshuo’s landscapes are endlessly photogenic, and with this travel map, you’re ready to capture its magic. Whether you’re framing the perfect shot or simply soaking in the views, every corner of this region tells a story.
