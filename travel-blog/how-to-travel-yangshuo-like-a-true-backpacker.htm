How to Travel Yangshuo Like a True Backpacker

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Yangshuo is not just a destination; it’s a rite of passage for backpackers. Nestled in Guangxi Province, this small town has been drawing adventurers for decades with its surreal karst mountains, winding rivers, and vibrant culture. But experiencing Yangshuo like a true backpacker isn’t about ticking off tourist traps—it’s about immersion, spontaneity, and connecting with the soul of this magical place. Here’s how to do it right.

Getting There and Around

Most backpackers start their journey from Guilin, which has an international airport and major train connections. From Guilin, skip the expensive taxis and hop on a local bus to Yangshuo—it’s cheap, takes about 1.5 hours, and offers stunning views of the countryside. Once in Yangshuo, forget about cars. Rent a bicycle or an electric scooter (around 30-50 RMB per day) to explore at your own pace. Walking is also a great way to soak in the atmosphere, especially along the Li River.

Where to Stay: Hostels and Guesthouses

True backpackers know that accommodation is more than just a place to sleep—it’s where you meet fellow travelers and swap stories. Yangshuo is filled with budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses. Look for spots like the Yangshuo Backpacker Hostel or Riverside Lodge, where dorm beds cost as little as 40 RMB per night. These places often organize group activities, from rock climbing to cooking classes, making it easy to find adventure buddies. For a more local experience, consider staying in a nearby village like Xingping or Fuli, where family-run guesthouses offer authentic hospitality.

Exploring the Natural Wonders

Cycling Through the Karst Landscape

One of the best ways to experience Yangshuo is on two wheels. Rent a bike and head out to the countryside. The route from Yangshuo to the Yulong River is a favorite, passing through rice paddies, bamboo groves, and tiny villages. Stop along the way to take photos, chat with farmers, or simply enjoy the silence broken only by birdsong. Don’t miss the iconic Moon Hill—a natural arch with a hike that rewards you with panoramic views.

Bamboo Rafting on the Yulong River

While the Li River gets all the hype, the Yulong River offers a more peaceful and backpacker-friendly alternative. Skip the crowded tourist boats and opt for a bamboo raft. You can even combine it with a cycling trip—many rental shops will arrange for your bike to be transported downstream. Drift past water buffaloes, ancient bridges, and lush greenery. It’s a quintessential Yangshuo experience that feels worlds away from the hustle of the town center.

Hiking and Rock Climbing

For the adventurous, Yangshuo is a paradise. The karst mountains are perfect for hiking and rock climbing. Even if you’re a beginner, there are plenty of guided tours available. Hike up to the top of Xianggong Hill for sunrise—it’s less crowded than other spots and offers breathtaking views of the Li River winding through the peaks. If you’re into rock climbing, join a half-day session with a local guide. Yangshuo has routes for all levels, and climbing these limestone cliffs is an unforgettable thrill.

Diving into Local Culture

Learning to Cook Guilin Cuisine

Food is a huge part of the backpacking experience, and Yangshuo doesn’t disappoint. Take a cooking class to learn how to make local dishes like beer fish (píjiǔ yú), Guilin noodles (Guìlín mǐfěn), or stuffed Li River snails. Many hostels offer classes, or you can book one through a local family. It’s not just about eating—it’s about understanding the culture through its flavors.

Exploring Xingping Ancient Town

A short bus ride from Yangshuo, Xingping is a charming ancient town that feels frozen in time. Wander through its narrow streets, admire the Ming and Qing dynasty architecture, and visit the old fishing village. This is also where you’ll find the spot featured on the 20 RMB note—a must-do photo op for every backpacker. For a truly authentic experience, stay overnight in Xingping and wake up to the sound of the river.

Visiting Local Markets

To see the real Yangshuo, head to the local markets. The morning market in Yangshuo town is a bustling affair where farmers sell fresh produce, spices, and street food. Try some youtiao (fried dough sticks) or a bowl of hot doujiang (soy milk). For something unique, visit the Fuli Market, known for its handmade fans and paintings. It’s a great place to pick up souvenirs while supporting local artisans.

Nightlife and Social Scene

Backpacking isn’t just about daytime adventures—it’s also about the nights. Yangshuo has a lively nightlife centered around West Street (Xijie). This pedestrian street is packed with bars, cafes, and street performers. Grab a cheap beer at a roadside stall, join a pub quiz, or simply people-watch. For a more relaxed evening, many hostels organize bonfires, movie nights, or live music. It’s the perfect time to share travel stories and make new friends from around the world.

Budget Tips for the Frugal Traveler

Traveling like a true backpacker means stretching your money without missing out. Here are some tips: - Eat at local noodle shops instead of tourist restaurants—a bowl of Guilin noodles costs about 10 RMB. - Avoid packaged tours; explore independently or join group activities organized by hostels. - Travel during the shoulder seasons (spring or autumn) to avoid crowds and higher prices. - Bargain politely at markets, but remember that a few RMB means more to locals than to you.

Off-the-Beaten-Path Gems

While everyone visits the popular spots, true backpackers seek out hidden gems. Consider these: - The Dragon Bridge: An ancient stone bridge in the countryside, perfect for a quiet picnic. - Silver Cave: A less-visited alternative to the crowded Reed Flute Cave, with impressive stalactites. - Village Homestays: Spend a night in a village like Longji to experience rural life and terraced fields.

Embracing the Backpacker Spirit

Ultimately, traveling Yangshuo like a true backpacker is about attitude. Be open to spontaneity—say yes to that random invitation to join a local family for dinner or to hike a mountain at dawn. Talk to everyone: fellow travelers, hostel owners, and locals. They might lead you to experiences you never imagined. Leave room in your itinerary for getting lost, because in Yangshuo, the best discoveries often happen when you least expect them.

Yangshuo is a place that captures hearts. With its stunning landscape, rich culture, and welcoming community, it’s no wonder backpackers return year after year. So pack your bag, grab your sense of adventure, and go see it for yourself.
