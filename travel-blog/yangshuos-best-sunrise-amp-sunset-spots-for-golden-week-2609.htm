Yangshuo’s Best Sunrise & Sunset Spots for Golden Week

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Golden Week in China is one of the most anticipated travel periods of the year. As autumn sweeps across the country, Yangshuo emerges as a premier destination for travelers seeking natural beauty, adventure, and moments of pure tranquility. Famous for its karst mountains, the Li River, and vibrant culture, Yangshuo offers some of the most breathtaking sunrise and sunset views in the world. This guide will help you discover the best spots to witness the sky painted in hues of gold, orange, and pink during your Golden Week visit.

Why Yangshuo is Perfect for Golden Week

Golden Week, typically occurring in early October, brings pleasant weather to Yangshuo. The summer heat has faded, leaving behind crisp mornings and comfortable evenings perfect for outdoor activities. While it’s a busy travel period, the natural expanses of Yangshuo provide ample opportunities to escape the crowds and find solitude amidst stunning landscapes. Capturing sunrise and sunset here isn’t just about photography—it’s about experiencing the serene harmony between land and sky.

Top Sunrise Spots in Yangshuo

Waking up early to catch the sunrise is a ritual for many visitors in Yangshuo. The misty mornings, combined with the silhouettes of karst peaks, create a magical atmosphere that’s worth setting the alarm for.

Xianggong Mountain

Xianggong Mountain is arguably the most famous sunrise viewpoint in Yangshuo. Located about an hour’s drive from Yangshuo town, this spot requires a short but steep hike. The effort is rewarded with a panoramic vista of the Li River winding through a forest of karst hills. At dawn, the first rays of sunlight illuminate the river valley, creating a golden reflection that seems to set the world on fire. Arrive early during Golden Week to secure a good position, as this spot can get crowded with photographers and nature lovers.

Moon Hill

Moon Hill, known for its natural arch formation, is another excellent location for sunrise. The hike to the top takes about 20-30 minutes and offers several viewing platforms along the way. As the sun rises behind the karst formations, the play of light and shadow through the arch is a spectacular sight. It’s less crowded than Xianggong Mountain at dawn, making it a great alternative for those seeking a more peaceful experience.

Laozhai Mountain

For the adventurous traveler, Laozhai Mountain provides a challenging but rewarding sunrise hike. The trail is less developed, so it’s best to hire a local guide. From the summit, you’ll have a 360-degree view of Yangshuo’s countryside, with layers of misty mountains unfolding in every direction. The sunrise here feels like a private show, with the sun emerging from behind distant peaks and gradually lighting up the entire region.

Best Sunset Locations

As the day winds down, Yangshuo’s landscapes transform under the warm glow of the setting sun. From riverside spots to mountain tops, here are the best places to enjoy the evening spectacle.

Yulong River

The Yulong River is a tributary of the Li River and offers a more intimate setting for sunset. Bamboo rafts drift slowly along the calm waters, and the surrounding farmland and villages take on a golden hue. Find a spot along the riverbank, or even better, rent a bamboo raft for a floating sunset experience. The reflections of the karst mountains on the water as the sun dips below the horizon are simply unforgettable.

West Street Rooftop Bars

For those who prefer a more relaxed vibe, the rooftop bars and cafes along West Street provide a charming urban sunset view. As the sun sets behind the mountains, the sky turns into a canvas of pastel colors, and the lively atmosphere of West Street begins to buzz. Enjoy a local beer or a cup of tea while watching the day transition into night. It’s a perfect blend of natural beauty and cultural excitement.

Fuli Bridge

Fuli Bridge is an ancient stone bridge that spans the Li River. It’s a popular spot for photographers aiming to capture the classic Yangshuo sunset. The combination of the historic bridge, flowing river, and towering karst peaks creates a timeless scene. As the sun sets, the sky often explodes in vibrant colors, reflecting off the water and adding drama to the landscape. It’s easily accessible and suitable for all types of travelers.

Photography Tips for Golden Light

Capturing the perfect sunrise or sunset photo in Yangshuo requires some preparation. Here are a few tips to make the most of the golden hour:

Gear Recommendations

Bring a tripod to stabilize your camera for long exposures, especially during the low light of dawn and dusk. A wide-angle lens is ideal for capturing the vast landscapes, while a telephoto lens can help isolate details like the sun behind a karst peak. Don’t forget extra batteries and memory cards—you’ll need them!

Timing is Everything

Arrive at least 30-45 minutes before sunrise or sunset to scout the location and set up your equipment. The light changes rapidly during golden hour, so be ready to adjust your settings frequently. Use apps like PhotoPills or The Photographer’s Ephemeris to plan the sun’s position in advance.

Composition Ideas

Incorporate elements like rivers, trees, or traditional boats to add depth and context to your photos. Silhouettes of karst mountains against the colorful sky are always effective. Experiment with different angles and perspectives to create unique shots.

Beyond Sunrise and Sunset: Making the Most of Golden Week

While sunrise and sunset are highlights, Yangshuo has much more to offer during Golden Week. Here are some additional activities to enrich your trip:

Cycling Through the Countryside

Rent a bicycle and explore the flat, scenic paths that wind through rice paddies, local villages, and along the rivers. It’s a fantastic way to experience the rural beauty of Yangshuo at your own pace.

Rock Climbing Adventures

Yangshuo is a world-renowned rock climbing destination. The karst mountains provide countless routes for climbers of all levels. Join a guided climbing tour to see the landscape from a completely different perspective.

Local Cuisine and Culture

Don’t miss out on Yangshuo’s culinary delights. Try local specialties like beer fish, rice noodles, and stuffed Li River snails. Visit the night markets or take a cooking class to learn how to prepare these dishes yourself.

Navigating Crowds During Golden Week

Golden Week is one of the busiest travel times in China, so planning ahead is crucial. Book accommodations and transportation early to avoid last-minute hassles. Consider visiting popular spots at off-peak hours, such as very early in the morning or later in the evening. Exploring lesser-known areas can also provide a more authentic and crowd-free experience.

Sustainable Travel in Yangshuo

As tourism grows, it’s important to travel responsibly. Respect the natural environment by staying on marked trails, carrying out trash, and avoiding plastic waste. Support local businesses by eating at family-owned restaurants and hiring local guides. Your mindful travel helps preserve Yangshuo’s beauty for future generations.

Yangshuo’s sunrises and sunsets are more than just visual spectacles—they are moments of connection with nature’s grandeur. Whether you’re standing atop a mountain at dawn or watching the sun dip below the horizon from a riverside retreat, these experiences define the magic of Yangshuo. This Golden Week, embark on a journey to witness these unforgettable scenes and create memories that will last a lifetime.
