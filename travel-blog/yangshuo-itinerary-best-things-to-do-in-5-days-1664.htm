Yangshuo Itinerary: Best Things to Do in 5 Days

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Yangshuo, nestled in the heart of Guangxi Province, is a dream destination for travelers seeking breathtaking karst landscapes, vibrant culture, and outdoor adventures. This 5-day itinerary will guide you through the must-see spots, hidden gems, and unforgettable experiences that make Yangshuo one of China’s top travel hotspots.

Day 1: Arrival & Exploring Yangshuo Town

Morning: Arrive and Settle In

Start your journey by checking into a boutique hotel or guesthouse in Yangshuo Town. Popular options include the Yangshuo Mountain Retreat or Secret Garden, both offering stunning views of the surrounding karst peaks.

Afternoon: Wander Through West Street

Head to West Street (Xi Jie), the bustling heart of Yangshuo. This pedestrian-friendly street is lined with cafes, souvenir shops, and vibrant street art. Don’t miss:
- Trying local snacks like beer fish (a Yangshuo specialty) or stuffed Li River snails.
- Sipping coffee at Mingyuan Coffee, a cozy spot with rooftop views.

Evening: Impression Sanjie Liu Light Show

Cap off your first night with the Impression Sanjie Liu, a mesmerizing outdoor performance directed by Zhang Yimou. Set against the Li River, this show combines traditional music, dance, and stunning light effects.

Day 2: Li River Cruise & Xingping Village

Morning: Li River Cruise from Yangdi to Xingping

Embark on a bamboo raft or boat cruise along the Li River, passing iconic scenery like Nine Horses Fresco Hill and Yellow Cloth Shoal. The stretch from Yangdi to Xingping is the most picturesque.

Afternoon: Explore Xingping Village

Disembark in Xingping, a charming ancient village with well-preserved Qing Dynasty architecture. Highlights include:
- Climbing Laozhai Mountain for a panoramic view of the Li River’s bend.
- Visiting the 20 RMB Note viewpoint, where the river’s landscape matches China’s famous banknote.

Evening: Return to Yangshuo

Head back to Yangshuo and enjoy dinner at Cloud 9, a rooftop restaurant with killer views.

Day 3: Countryside Cycling & Yulong River

Morning: Bike Through the Countryside

Rent a bike and pedal through Yangshuo’s idyllic countryside. A popular route includes:
- Moon Hill: Hike up for a stunning view through its natural arch.
- Butterfly Spring Park: A scenic spot with caves and lush greenery.

Afternoon: Bamboo Rafting on Yulong River

Drift down the Yulong River on a bamboo raft, passing under ancient stone bridges and terraced fields. The Dragon Bridge area is particularly photogenic.

Evening: Cooking Class at a Local Farm

Join a farm-to-table cooking class to learn how to make Guilin rice noodles or stir-fried river fish. Many classes include a market tour to pick fresh ingredients.

Day 4: Adventure Day: Caving & Rock Climbing

Morning: Explore the Silver Cave

Venture into Silver Cave (Yinzi Yan), a glittering limestone cavern filled with stalactites and underground rivers. The walkways are well-lit, making it accessible for all ages.

Afternoon: Rock Climbing in the Karsts

Yangshuo is a world-class rock climbing destination. Beginners can try spots like The Egg or White Mountain, while advanced climbers tackle Butterfly Peak. Local guides offer half-day tours with gear included.

Evening: Drinks at a Riverside Bar

Unwind at Mojo Bar or Lizard Lounge, both popular among expats and travelers for their laid-back vibe and live music.

Day 5: Relaxation & Hidden Gems

Morning: Sunrise at Xianggong Hill

Wake up early for a sunrise hike up Xianggong Hill. The 20-minute climb rewards you with a breathtaking view of the Li River winding through the karsts.

Afternoon: Visit Fuli Town

Take a short trip to Fuli Town, known for its handmade painted fans and tranquil riverside setting. Stroll along the old streets and watch artisans at work.

Evening: Farewell Dinner with a View

End your trip with a meal at Giggling Tree, a farmhouse restaurant serving organic dishes amidst rice paddies.

Yangshuo’s magic lies in its blend of adventure, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you’re rafting down the Yulong River or sipping coffee on West Street, this 5-day itinerary ensures an unforgettable journey.
