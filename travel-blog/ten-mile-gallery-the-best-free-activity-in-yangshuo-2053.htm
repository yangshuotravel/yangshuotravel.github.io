Ten Mile Gallery: The Best Free Activity in Yangshuo

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Yangshuo is a dream destination for travelers seeking breathtaking landscapes, vibrant culture, and unforgettable adventures. While the Li River cruise and the iconic karst mountains often steal the spotlight, there’s one hidden gem that offers an equally mesmerizing experience—without costing a dime. Welcome to Ten Mile Gallery, the best free activity in Yangshuo.

Why Ten Mile Gallery is a Must-Visit

Nestled along the scenic road between Yangshuo and the nearby village of Gaotian, Ten Mile Gallery is a 10-kilometer stretch of pure natural beauty. The name itself hints at what awaits: a picturesque "gallery" of towering limestone peaks, lush rice paddies, and serene countryside vistas. Unlike crowded tourist spots, this area offers a peaceful escape where you can immerse yourself in the raw charm of rural Guangxi.

The Scenic Route

The best way to experience Ten Mile Gallery is by bike or electric scooter. Renting one is cheap and easy in Yangshuo, and the flat, well-paved road makes it accessible for riders of all skill levels. As you pedal or glide along, you’ll pass by:

  • Karst Mountain Panoramas: The jagged peaks rise dramatically from the earth, creating a surreal backdrop at every turn.
  • Golden Rice Fields: Depending on the season, the paddies shimmer in shades of green or gold, offering perfect photo ops.
  • Quaint Villages: Stop by small settlements like Jiuxian or Chaolong to glimpse local life and maybe even chat with friendly farmers.

Hidden Gems Along the Way

While the entire route is stunning, a few spots stand out as must-see highlights.

Moon Hill

Just a short detour from the main road, Moon Hill is an iconic natural arch formed by centuries of erosion. The hike to the top is steep but rewarding, with panoramic views of the surrounding countryside. If you’re not up for the climb, even the view from the base is impressive.

Big Banyan Tree

This 1,400-year-old tree is a living piece of history. Its massive canopy provides shade for a small park, making it a great spot to rest and soak in the atmosphere. Local legends say it’s a symbol of longevity and prosperity.

Yulong River Bamboo Rafting (Nearby Alternative)

While not part of Ten Mile Gallery itself, the Yulong River is just a stone’s throw away. If you’re willing to splurge a little, a bamboo raft ride here is a serene complement to your biking adventure. The calm waters and reflections of the karst mountains are pure magic.

Tips for the Perfect Visit

To make the most of your Ten Mile Gallery experience, keep these tips in mind:

  • Go Early or Late: The light is best at sunrise or sunset, and you’ll avoid the midday heat.
  • Pack Light Snacks: There are small roadside stalls, but bringing water and snacks ensures you stay fueled.
  • Respect the Locals: This is a working rural area—smile, be polite, and avoid trampling crops for photos.

Ten Mile Gallery proves that the best things in life (and travel) are often free. Whether you’re a photographer, nature lover, or just someone craving an authentic slice of Yangshuo, this scenic route belongs at the top of your itinerary.
