Must-See Yangshuo: The Best Views & Photo Spots

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Yangshuo, nestled in the heart of Guangxi, is a dream destination for travelers and photographers alike. With its dramatic karst mountains, winding rivers, and vibrant local culture, this small town offers endless opportunities for breathtaking shots and unforgettable experiences. Whether you're an adventurer, a shutterbug, or simply someone who appreciates natural beauty, Yangshuo has something for everyone. Here’s your ultimate guide to the best views and photo spots in Yangshuo.

The Iconic Karst Landscapes

1. Xianggong Mountain

For the most jaw-dropping sunrise or sunset views, Xianggong Mountain is unbeatable. A short but steep hike (about 15-20 minutes) leads you to a panoramic viewpoint overlooking the Li River’s dramatic bends, framed by towering karst peaks. Arrive early to secure a prime spot—this location is a favorite among photographers.

2. Moon Hill

This natural limestone arch is one of Yangshuo’s most recognizable landmarks. The hike to the top takes about 30-45 minutes, but the view from the summit is worth every step. For a unique perspective, capture the arch from the base or frame it against a golden sunset.

3. Ten-Mile Gallery

A scenic route stretching from Yangshuo to Gaotian, the Ten-Mile Gallery is best explored by bike or electric scooter. The road winds through rice paddies, quaint villages, and towering karst formations. Stop at viewpoints like Butterfly Spring or Big Banyan Tree for postcard-perfect shots.

The Li & Yulong Rivers

4. Li River Bamboo Rafting (Xingping to Yangdi)

The stretch between Xingping and Yangdi is famously depicted on the 20 RMB note. A bamboo raft ride here offers unobstructed views of the river’s serene waters and the iconic Nine Horses Fresco Hill. For the best lighting, go in the early morning or late afternoon.

5. Yulong River Bamboo Rafts

Less crowded than the Li River, the Yulong River is perfect for a peaceful float. The Dragon Bridge area is particularly photogenic, with traditional stone bridges and lush greenery reflecting in the calm waters.

6. Xingping Ancient Town

This 1,500-year-old fishing village is a treasure trove of photo ops. Wander the cobbled streets, snap shots of weathered wooden boats along the riverbank, or climb Laozhai Mountain for a bird’s-eye view of the Li River’s horseshoe bend.

Hidden Gems & Local Life

7. Fuli Town’s Hand-Painted Fans

A short drive from Yangshuo, Fuli is famous for its handmade painted fans. Visit local workshops to see artisans at work—these colorful, intricate designs make for unique cultural shots.

8. Silver Cave

A mesmerizing underground world of stalactites and stalagmites, Silver Cave is a cool escape on hot days. The illuminated rock formations create surreal photo opportunities, especially in the cavern’s grand halls.

9. Yangshuo West Street at Night

By day, West Street is a bustling hub of cafes and souvenir shops. At night, it transforms into a neon-lit spectacle. Capture the lively atmosphere, street performers, and glowing lanterns for vibrant urban shots.

Adventure & Aerial Views

10. Hot Air Balloon Rides

For a bird’s-eye perspective, take a hot air balloon ride at dawn. Floating above the karst peaks and patchwork of rice fields is an unforgettable experience—and the photos are even better.

11. Rock Climbing at Swiss Cheese Rock

Yangshuo is a global rock-climbing hotspot. Swiss Cheese Rock, with its unique perforated limestone, offers thrilling climbs and stunning backdrops. Even if you’re not climbing, the area is great for action shots.

12. Paragliding Over the Countryside

For adrenaline junkies, paragliding provides unparalleled aerial views. Launch from Lotus Peak and soar over the Yulong River valley, capturing the landscape from above.

Seasonal Highlights

13. Spring: Rapeseed Flower Fields

In March and April, the countryside bursts into yellow as rapeseed flowers bloom. The contrast between the bright flowers and jagged karst peaks is a photographer’s dream.

14. Summer: Rice Terraces in Longsheng

While not in Yangshuo proper, the Longji Rice Terraces (a 2-hour drive away) are a must-visit in summer. The layered paddies resemble dragon scales, especially at sunrise.

15. Autumn: Golden Sunset at Moon Hill

As temperatures cool, the light softens, casting a golden glow over Moon Hill. The surrounding countryside, dotted with persimmon trees, adds warm hues to your shots.

16. Winter: Misty River Mornings

Winter brings ethereal fog that blankets the Li River, creating a mystical atmosphere. Early mornings offer hauntingly beautiful shots of shrouded karst peaks.

Pro Tips for Photography

  • Golden Hours: Shoot at sunrise (5-7 AM) or sunset (5-7 PM) for soft, warm lighting.
  • Drones: Check local regulations—some areas restrict drone use, but permitted spots yield stunning aerial footage.
  • Local Guides: Hire a guide for off-the-beaten-path spots like hidden waterfalls or secret viewpoints.
  • Gear: A wide-angle lens is ideal for landscapes, while a zoom lens helps capture distant karst details.

Yangshuo’s beauty is timeless, and every corner offers a new frame-worthy moment. Whether you’re chasing the perfect sunrise or immersing yourself in rural charm, this guide ensures you won’t miss a single must-see vista. Happy shooting!
