Yangshuo’s Must-See Festivals & Events

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Nestled in the heart of Guangxi’s breathtaking karst landscape, Yangshuo is not just a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts but also a cultural hub teeming with vibrant festivals and events. Whether you’re a thrill-seeker, a culture buff, or a foodie, Yangshuo’s annual celebrations offer something unforgettable. Here’s your ultimate guide to the must-see festivals and events that should be on every traveler’s radar.

Yangshuo’s Top Cultural Festivals

1. Yangshuo International Music Festival

Held every spring, the Yangshuo International Music Festival transforms the town into a global stage. Musicians from China and beyond gather to perform everything from folk to rock, jazz to electronic. The open-air venues, often set against the backdrop of the Li River or Moon Hill, create an otherworldly experience.

Highlights:
- Free performances in iconic locations like West Street and Xingping.
- Workshops where visitors can learn traditional Chinese instruments.
- Late-night jam sessions at local bars.

2. Sanjie Liu Light Show

Inspired by the legendary folk singer Sanjie Liu, this open-air light and sound spectacle is a visual masterpiece. Directed by Zhang Yimou (of Olympics Opening Ceremony fame), the show uses the Li River as its stage, with hundreds of performers and dazzling projections on the karst mountains.

Why It’s Unmissable:
- The world’s largest natural theater.
- A fusion of Zhuang minority culture and modern technology.
- Nighttime boat rides for the best views.

Adventure & Sports Events

3. Yangshuo Rock Climbing Festival

Yangshuo is Asia’s rock-climbing mecca, and this annual festival (usually in November) draws climbers of all levels. With over 400 bolted routes, the limestone cliffs offer challenges for beginners and pros alike.

What to Expect:
- Guided climbs at spots like Moon Hill and Swiss Cheese Rock.
- Gear demos from top outdoor brands.
- Post-climb parties with live music.

4. Li River Marathon

For runners who crave scenery, the Li River Marathon is a dream come true. The route winds through rice paddies, bamboo groves, and riverside trails, making it one of the most picturesque races in China.

Race Tips:
- Sign up early—spots fill fast!
- Train for mixed terrain (some steep trails).
- Stay hydrated; Guangxi’s humidity can be intense.

Food & Seasonal Celebrations

5. Yangshuo Beer Fish Festival

No trip to Yangshuo is complete without trying beer fish (píjiǔ yú), the local specialty. This summer festival celebrates the dish with cooking competitions, street stalls, and endless tastings.

Must-Try Dishes:
- Beer fish (carp simmered in local brew).
- Stuffed Li River snails.
- Bamboo rice cooked in hollow bamboo tubes.

6. Mid-Autumn Festival Lantern Parade

During China’s Mid-Autumn Festival, Yangshuo glows with lantern-lit boats floating down the Li River. Locals and visitors release sky lanterns, creating a magical nightscape.

Best Spots to Celebrate:
- Moon Hill for lantern-lit hikes.
- West Street for mooncake tastings.
- Private river cruises for an intimate experience.

Off-the-Beaten-Path Gems

7. Yangshuo Countryside Bike Fest

Escape the crowds and pedal through Yangshuo’s rural heartland during this springtime event. Farmers open their doors for homestays, and routes pass hidden caves and ancient bridges.

Bike Routes to Explore:
- Yulong River Loop (easy, scenic).
- Xingping to Langshi Village (moderate, historic).
- Butterfly Spring Trail (challenging, lush).

8. Zhuang Minority New Year

While not as touristy as other events, the Zhuang New Year (usually in April) offers a rare glimpse into local traditions. Expect dragon dances, buffalo fights (symbolic, not violent!), and feasts with five-color sticky rice.

Cultural Insights:
- Wear red for luck—it’s the Zhuang lucky color.
- Join in the bamboo pole dance if you dare!
- Try oil tea, a savory local brew.

Planning Your Trip

When to Go:
- Spring (March–May): Music festivals, mild weather.
- Summer (June–August): Beer Fish Fest, but prepare for heat.
- Fall (September–November): Climbing, marathons, clear skies.
- Winter (December–February): Quieter, but still pleasant.

Pro Tips:
- Book accommodations early for major festivals.
- Rent an e-bike to explore at your own pace.
- Pack layers—Yangshuo’s weather can shift fast.

From moonlit lanterns to adrenaline-pumping climbs, Yangshuo’s festivals are as diverse as its landscapes. Whether you’re here for the culture, the adventure, or the food, these events will make your trip unforgettable.
