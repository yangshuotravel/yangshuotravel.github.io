Yangshuo’s Li River vs. Yulong River: Which One to Choose?

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

When planning a trip to Yangshuo, Guangxi, one of the most common dilemmas travelers face is whether to explore the iconic Li River or the serene Yulong River. Both offer breathtaking karst landscapes, but they cater to different tastes and travel styles. Whether you're an adventure seeker, a photography enthusiast, or someone looking for a peaceful retreat, understanding the differences between these two rivers will help you make the best choice for your journey.

The Legendary Li River: A Must-See for First-Timers

Why the Li River is Famous

The Li River is the poster child of Guilin’s natural beauty, often featured in Chinese paintings and travel brochures. Its dramatic limestone peaks, misty waters, and traditional fishing villages have made it a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The most famous section is between Guilin and Yangshuo, where a leisurely cruise offers uninterrupted views of the surreal landscape.

Best Ways to Experience the Li River

  • Scenic Cruise: The classic way to see the Li River is by taking a 4-5 hour cruise from Guilin to Yangshuo. Boats range from budget-friendly options to luxury vessels with open-air decks.
  • Bamboo Rafting: For a more intimate experience, shorter bamboo raft rides are available near Yangshuo, particularly around the Xingping area, where you’ll find the iconic view featured on the 20 RMB note.
  • Hiking & Photography: The riverside trails near Xingping and Yangdi are perfect for hikers and photographers who want to capture the scenery at their own pace.

Downsides of the Li River

  • Crowds: The Li River is extremely popular, especially during peak seasons (April-October). Expect packed boats and busy viewpoints.
  • Commercialization: Some sections are lined with souvenir stalls and loud tour groups, which can detract from the natural serenity.

The Hidden Gem: Yulong River

Why Yulong River is a Local Favorite

While the Li River gets all the fame, the Yulong River is where many locals and seasoned travelers go for a quieter, more authentic experience. This smaller river winds through lush countryside, ancient bridges, and small villages, offering a glimpse of rural Guangxi life.

Best Ways to Enjoy the Yulong River

  • Bamboo Rafting: Unlike the motorized rafts on the Li River, Yulong’s bamboo rafts are manually poled, creating a peaceful, slow-paced journey. The route from Jinlong Bridge to Gongnong Bridge is particularly scenic.
  • Cycling Along the Riverbank: Rent a bike in Yangshuo and follow the well-marked paths that run alongside the Yulong. You’ll pass rice paddies, water buffalo, and charming farmhouses.
  • Swimming & Picnicking: In summer, the shallow, clear waters of the Yulong are perfect for a refreshing dip. Locals often picnic by the riverbanks.

Downsides of the Yulong River

  • Limited Infrastructure: There are fewer restaurants and facilities compared to the Li River, so bring snacks and water if you’re planning a full-day excursion.
  • Less Dramatic Scenery: While beautiful, the Yulong lacks the towering peaks that make the Li River so iconic.

Key Differences: Li River vs. Yulong River

For Photography Enthusiasts

  • Li River: Best for grand, sweeping landscapes (sunrise at Huangbu Shoal, the 20 RMB viewpoint).
  • Yulong River: Ideal for intimate shots—old stone bridges, reflections in calm waters, rural life.

For Adventure Seekers

  • Li River: Offers rock climbing near Xingping and longer hiking trails.
  • Yulong River: Great for cycling, swimming, and off-the-beaten-path exploration.

For Relaxation

  • Li River: Can feel hectic unless you opt for a premium cruise.
  • Yulong River: Unmatched tranquility, especially early in the morning or late afternoon.

Seasonal Considerations

  • Spring (March-May): Both rivers are lush and misty, but the Li River may be crowded during holidays like Labor Day.
  • Summer (June-August): Yulong’s shallow waters are perfect for cooling off, while the Li River can feel overcrowded.
  • Autumn (September-November): Ideal for clear skies and comfortable temperatures on both rivers.
  • Winter (December-February): Fewer tourists, but some rafting services may be limited due to colder weather.

Budget & Accessibility

  • Li River: Cruises can be pricey (200-500 RMB), and transportation from Guilin adds to the cost.
  • Yulong River: More budget-friendly; bamboo rafting costs around 150-200 RMB, and biking is cheap or free.

Final Thoughts

If it’s your first time in Yangshuo, the Li River is a must—it’s the quintessential Guangxi experience. But if you prefer a slower, more immersive escape, the Yulong River will steal your heart. Better yet? Visit both and see why this region is one of China’s most beloved destinations.
