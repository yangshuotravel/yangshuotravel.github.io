Yangshuo’s Best Spots for a Long-Term Stay

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Yangshuo, nestled in the heart of Guangxi’s karst landscape, is a dream destination for travelers seeking both adventure and tranquility. While many visit for a short getaway, those who stay longer discover a deeper connection to its natural beauty, vibrant culture, and laid-back lifestyle. Whether you’re a digital nomad, an artist, or simply someone looking to escape the hustle and bustle, Yangshuo offers countless spots perfect for an extended stay. Here’s a curated list of the best areas to settle in for a while.

The Riverside Retreat: Xingping

Why Xingping?

Xingping, a picturesque riverside town about an hour from Yangshuo’s main tourist hub, is where time seems to slow down. Famous for its postcard-perfect views of the Li River and the iconic 20 RMB note scenery, Xingping is ideal for those who crave serenity without sacrificing convenience.

Long-Term Stay Perks

  • Authentic Local Life: Unlike the busier Yangshuo town, Xingping retains a more traditional vibe. You’ll wake up to fishermen casting their nets and farmers tending to their fields.
  • Outdoor Adventures: The area is a gateway to hiking trails like Laozhai Mountain, offering panoramic views of the karst peaks. Kayaking and bamboo rafting are also easily accessible.
  • Cozy Accommodations: From boutique guesthouses to riverside homestays, Xingping has plenty of options for extended stays, many with stunning balcony views.

Insider Tip

Visit the local market early in the morning for fresh produce and street food. The youcha (oil tea), a regional specialty, is a must-try.

The Digital Nomad Hub: West Street (Xi Jie)

Why West Street?

Yangshuo’s West Street is the beating heart of the town, blending Chinese culture with a cosmopolitan flair. For remote workers and social butterflies, this area offers the perfect mix of work and play.

Long-Term Stay Perks

  • Coworking Spaces: Cafés like Mojo Coffee and The Giggling Tree cater to digital nomads with reliable Wi-Fi and a creative atmosphere.
  • Nightlife and Networking: West Street’s bars and live music venues make it easy to meet fellow travelers and expats.
  • Convenience: Supermarkets, international restaurants, and bike rental shops are all within walking distance.

Insider Tip

For a quieter work environment, head to Secret Garden, a café tucked away in a traditional courtyard just off the main strip.

The Countryside Escape: Yulong River Area

Why Yulong River?

If you’re after a peaceful retreat surrounded by rice paddies and bamboo groves, the Yulong River area is your best bet. This idyllic countryside is perfect for nature lovers and those seeking inspiration.

Long-Term Stay Perks

  • Bike-Friendly Trails: The flat terrain along the river is ideal for cycling, with rental shops offering affordable monthly rates.
  • Riverside Cottages: Many guesthouses here feature private terraces overlooking the water, perfect for morning yoga or evening stargazing.
  • Local Experiences: Join a cooking class at a nearby farm or learn traditional crafts like bamboo weaving from villagers.

Insider Tip

Rent a bamboo raft for a leisurely float down the Yulong River—it’s far less crowded than the Li River and just as scenic.

The Hidden Gem: Fuli Town

Why Fuli?

Fuli, a small town east of Yangshuo, is famous for its handmade fans and painted umbrellas. It’s a haven for artists and creatives looking to immerse themselves in local craftsmanship.

Long-Term Stay Perks

  • Artistic Community: Workshops and studios welcome visitors to try their hand at fan painting or pottery.
  • Affordable Living: Accommodations here are cheaper than in Yangshuo town, making it a budget-friendly option.
  • Riverside Walks: The quiet paths along the Li River are perfect for solitary reflection or sketching.

Insider Tip

Visit during the Fuli Market Day (held every 3rd, 6th, and 9th day of the lunar calendar) for a lively glimpse of rural life.

The Adventurer’s Base: Moon Hill Village

Why Moon Hill Village?

Named after the iconic Moon Hill, this area is a hotspot for climbers and outdoor enthusiasts. If you thrive on adrenaline and stunning vistas, this is the place to be.

Long-Term Stay Perks

  • Rock Climbing Mecca: With over 300 climbing routes, Yangshuo is Asia’s premier climbing destination. Local guides offer lessons for all skill levels.
  • Hiking Trails: The hike up Moon Hill rewards you with a breathtaking view of the valley.
  • Eco-Lodges: Stay in sustainable accommodations that blend seamlessly with the environment.

Insider Tip

Join a sunrise hike to avoid the crowds and experience the karst landscape in golden light.

The Cultural Oasis: Longsheng Rice Terraces (Day Trips)

Why Longsheng?

While not in Yangshuo itself, the Longsheng Rice Terraces are close enough for weekend getaways. These cascading terraces are a UNESCO-listed wonder and a must-see for long-term visitors.

Long-Term Stay Perks

  • Seasonal Beauty: Each season paints the terraces in different hues—emerald green in summer, golden in autumn, and frosty white in winter.
  • Ethnic Culture: The area is home to the Zhuang and Yao minorities, offering rich cultural exchanges.
  • Hot Springs: After a day of hiking, unwind in the natural hot springs of Longsheng.

Insider Tip

Stay overnight in a traditional wooden guesthouse to witness the terraces at sunrise without the day-tour crowds.

Final Thoughts on Choosing Your Spot

Yangshuo’s diverse neighborhoods cater to every type of long-term traveler. Whether you’re drawn to the lively energy of West Street, the artistic charm of Fuli, or the rugged beauty of Moon Hill, there’s a perfect spot waiting for you. The key is to explore, connect with locals, and let the rhythm of Yangshuo guide your stay.
