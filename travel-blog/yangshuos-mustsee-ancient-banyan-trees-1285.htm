Yangshuo’s Must-See Ancient Banyan Trees

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Nestled in the heart of Guangxi’s karst landscape, Yangshuo is a destination that captivates travelers with its surreal scenery, winding rivers, and vibrant culture. But beyond the postcard-perfect views of the Li River and the bustling West Street, there’s a hidden natural wonder that often goes unnoticed—the ancient banyan trees. These towering giants, some over a thousand years old, are living monuments to Yangshuo’s rich history and ecological heritage.

The Legend of the Big Banyan Tree

One of the most famous ancient banyan trees in Yangshuo is the Big Banyan Tree (大榕树), located just a short bike ride from the town center. This colossal tree is estimated to be over 1,400 years old, with sprawling roots and a canopy that stretches nearly 60 feet in diameter.

A Symbol of Love and Longevity

Local legends say that the Big Banyan Tree was planted during the Tang Dynasty and has since become a symbol of eternal love. Couples often visit to tie red ribbons around its branches, hoping for blessings in their relationships. The tree’s massive roots, which seem to grow endlessly, are said to represent unbreakable bonds—making it a must-visit for romantics and photographers alike.

How to Visit

The tree is easily accessible by bike or electric scooter, and there’s a small entrance fee (around 20 RMB). Early mornings or late afternoons are the best times to visit, as the soft light filtering through the leaves creates a magical atmosphere.

Moon Hill’s Hidden Banyan Grove

While Moon Hill is famous for its crescent-shaped karst formation, few know about the ancient banyan trees that thrive at its base. These trees, some centuries old, form a serene grove perfect for a quiet retreat.

A Hiker’s Sanctuary

After climbing the 800 steps to Moon Hill’s summit, many travelers descend to relax under the shade of these banyans. The grove offers a cool respite from the sun, and the rustling leaves create a soothing soundtrack—ideal for meditation or a picnic.

Eco-Tourism and Conservation

Local efforts are underway to protect these ancient trees from over-tourism. Visitors are encouraged to stay on marked paths and avoid carving into the bark, ensuring these natural wonders endure for future generations.

The Banyan Trees of Yulong River

The Yulong River region is another hotspot for ancient banyans, particularly along the scenic bamboo raft route. These trees often grow right at the water’s edge, their roots dipping into the river like nature’s own sculptures.

Bamboo Rafting with a View

Many bamboo raft tours include stops near these majestic trees, allowing travelers to admire their grandeur up close. Some even feature small riverside cafes where you can sip tea while gazing at the banyans—a truly immersive experience.

Photography Tips

For the best shots, visit during golden hour when the sunlight reflects off the river, casting a warm glow on the trees. Wide-angle lenses work well to capture their full scale, while macro lenses can highlight intricate root details.

The Cultural Significance of Banyans in Yangshuo

Beyond their beauty, these trees hold deep cultural meaning. In Chinese tradition, banyans symbolize resilience and longevity—qualities that resonate with Yangshuo’s enduring charm.

Folklore and Festivals

During the annual Yangshuo Folk Festival, locals perform traditional dances and songs under the banyan trees, celebrating their heritage. These events offer a rare glimpse into rural Guangxi culture.

Spiritual Retreats

Some banyan groves are considered sacred, with small shrines placed at their bases. Visitors often leave offerings of fruit or incense, following centuries-old customs.

Sustainable Tourism: How to Respect These Ancient Giants

As more travelers discover Yangshuo’s banyan trees, responsible tourism becomes crucial. Here’s how you can help preserve them:

  • Stay on Designated Paths: Avoid trampling delicate root systems.
  • No Carvings or Graffiti: These trees are living history—leave them untouched.
  • Support Local Guides: Hiring knowledgeable guides ensures you learn about the trees without harming them.

Whether you’re a nature lover, a history buff, or simply seeking tranquility, Yangshuo’s ancient banyan trees offer an unforgettable experience. Their timeless presence is a reminder of the delicate balance between humanity and nature—one worth protecting for centuries to come.
