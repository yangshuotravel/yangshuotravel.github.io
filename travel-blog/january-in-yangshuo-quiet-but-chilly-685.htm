January in Yangshuo: Quiet but Chilly

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Yangshuo in January is a paradox—a place where the limestone karsts stand tall against a misty sky, where the Li River flows quietly, and where the usual bustle of tourists has faded into a serene hush. The air is crisp, the streets are less crowded, and the landscape takes on a dreamlike quality, wrapped in a chilly embrace. For those who don’t mind bundling up, this is the perfect time to experience Yangshuo in its most tranquil yet strikingly beautiful form.

Why Visit Yangshuo in January?

Most travelers associate Yangshuo with spring blossoms or autumn’s golden hues, but winter offers something entirely different. The crowds thin out, hotel rates drop, and the scenery takes on a mystical aura. The karst mountains, often shrouded in mist, look like something out of an ancient Chinese ink painting.

The Weather: Cold but Manageable

January is one of the coldest months in Yangshuo, with temperatures ranging from 5°C to 12°C (41°F to 54°F). Mornings can be particularly chilly, with fog lingering over the rivers and rice fields. However, the afternoons often bring weak but welcome sunlight. Packing layers is essential—thermal wear, a good jacket, and waterproof shoes will make exploring much more comfortable.

Fewer Tourists, More Authentic Experiences

Unlike peak seasons, January allows for a more intimate experience. Popular spots like Xingping Ancient Town or the Yulong River are free from the usual swarms of visitors. Cafés and restaurants are quieter, making it easier to strike up conversations with locals or fellow travelers.

Must-Do Activities in January

1. Biking Through the Countryside

Yangshuo’s countryside is breathtaking year-round, but winter adds a unique charm. Rent a bike and take the scenic route along the Yulong River or through the Ten-Mile Gallery. The air is fresh, the rice paddies are dormant, and the occasional mist makes the landscape feel surreal. Stop by small villages like Jiuxian or Fuli to see traditional rural life undisturbed by mass tourism.

2. A Bamboo Raft Ride on the Yulong River

While the Li River is the star attraction, the Yulong River offers a quieter, more peaceful alternative. A bamboo raft ride here in January is a magical experience—wrapped in blankets, drifting past misty karsts, with only the sound of water gently lapping against the raft. Some raft operators even provide hot tea to keep you warm.

3. Hiking Moon Hill or Xianggong Mountain

For those willing to brave the cold, hiking rewards with some of the best views in Yangshuo. Moon Hill’s iconic arch is even more striking against a winter sky, while Xianggong Mountain offers panoramic vistas of the Li River winding through the karsts. The trails are less crowded, so you can take your time soaking in the scenery.

4. Exploring Yangshuo’s Hidden Cafés

Yangshuo has a surprisingly vibrant café culture, and winter is the perfect time to enjoy it. Places like Mingyuan Coffee or Riverside Retreat offer cozy corners with heaters, great coffee, and often, stunning river views. It’s a great way to warm up after a morning of exploration.

Local Winter Delicacies to Try

1. Yangshuo Beer Fish (Pijiu Yu)

A local specialty, this dish features fresh river fish cooked in a savory beer-based broth with tomatoes, chili, and garlic. In winter, it’s especially comforting—spicy enough to warm you up but balanced with rich flavors.

2. Guilin Rice Noodles (Mifen)

Available year-round but particularly satisfying in cold weather, these noodles are served in a steaming hot broth with toppings like braised pork, peanuts, and pickled vegetables. Many small eateries in Yangshuo serve their own versions.

3. Hot Pot (Huoguo)

A winter staple, hot pot is a communal dining experience where you cook thinly sliced meats, vegetables, and tofu in a simmering pot of broth. Some restaurants in Yangshuo offer outdoor seating with heaters, making it a fun way to dine even in chilly weather.

Tips for Traveling in January

  • Dress in layers—mornings and evenings can be very cold, but midday might feel milder.
  • Check for early sunsets—daylight hours are shorter, so plan activities accordingly.
  • Book accommodations with heating—not all guesthouses in Yangshuo have proper heating, so confirm before booking.
  • Embrace the slow pace—winter is about quiet moments, so don’t rush. Sit by the river, sip tea, and just take it all in.

Yangshuo in January may not be the obvious choice, but for those who appreciate solitude, stunning landscapes, and a slower travel rhythm, it’s an unforgettable experience. The chill in the air only makes the warmth of the local hospitality—and the hot food—even more inviting.
