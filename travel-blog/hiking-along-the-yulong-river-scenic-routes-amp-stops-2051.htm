Hiking Along the Yulong River: Scenic Routes & Stops

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

The Yulong River, often overshadowed by its famous neighbor the Li River, is a hidden gem in Guangxi’s Yangshuo County. With its emerald waters, dramatic karst peaks, and serene countryside, it’s a paradise for hikers and nature lovers. Whether you’re a seasoned trekker or a casual walker, exploring the Yulong River on foot offers an intimate connection with China’s breathtaking landscapes. Here’s your ultimate guide to the best scenic routes and must-visit stops along the way.

Why Hike the Yulong River?

Unlike the crowded Li River, the Yulong River remains relatively untouched by mass tourism. The area is dotted with ancient bridges, rice paddies, and traditional villages, making it a perfect escape for those seeking authenticity. The river’s gentle flow and flat terrain also make it ideal for beginners, while the surrounding karst formations provide jaw-dropping backdrops at every turn.

Best Time to Visit

Spring (March-May) and autumn (September-November) are the prime seasons for hiking here. The weather is mild, and the landscapes are lush or golden, depending on the season. Summer can be hot and humid, while winter is cooler but still manageable for hiking.

Top Scenic Hiking Routes

1. Yulong River Bamboo Trail

This easy 5-kilometer trail follows the riverbank, weaving through bamboo groves and small farms. The path is well-marked and mostly flat, making it perfect for families or those looking for a relaxed stroll.

Highlights:
- Jiuxian Ancient Bridge: A 600-year-old stone bridge that’s one of the most photographed spots along the river.
- Local Farm Stalls: Stop by for fresh sugarcane juice or grilled river fish sold by villagers.

2. Fuli to Xingping Challenge

For more adventurous hikers, this 15-kilometer route connects two iconic towns. The trail includes river crossings, terraced fields, and panoramic viewpoints.

Highlights:
- Moon Hill: A short detour leads to this natural arch with sweeping views of the valley.
- Xingping Old Town: A charming fishing village that inspired the design on the 20 RMB note.

3. Dragon Bridge Loop

A moderate 8-kilometer loop starting at the iconic Dragon Bridge (Yulong Qiao). This route combines riverside paths with quiet village lanes.

Highlights:
- Dragon Bridge Sunset: Stay till dusk for a magical view of the bridge silhouetted against the karst peaks.
- Water Buffalo Encounters: Spot these gentle giants cooling off in the river.

Must-Visit Stops Along the Way

1. Jiuxian Ancient Bridge

This Ming Dynasty bridge is a masterpiece of rural architecture. Locals still use it daily, and it’s a great place to watch fishermen casting their nets.

2. Moon Hill

A short but steep climb rewards you with a 360-degree view of the Yulong Valley. The crescent-shaped hole in the mountain is a geological wonder.

3. Xingping’s Yellow Cloth Shoal

Famous for its reflection of the karst peaks on calm days, this spot is a photographer’s dream. Early mornings are the best time to visit.

4. Fuli’s Hand-Painted Fans

The town of Fuli is known for its traditional fan-making workshops. Watch artisans paint intricate designs on bamboo fans—a unique souvenir.

Tips for a Smooth Hike

  • Wear sturdy shoes: Some trails have uneven terrain or muddy patches after rain.
  • Pack light but smart: Bring water, snacks, sunscreen, and a rain jacket (weather can change quickly).
  • Respect local customs: Villages along the route are home to the Zhuang minority; always ask before taking photos of people.
  • Hire a local guide: For off-the-beaten-path routes, a guide can enhance the experience with stories and hidden gems.

Beyond Hiking: Other Yulong River Experiences

Bamboo Rafting

If your legs need a break, hop on a bamboo raft for a leisurely float downstream. The rafts are poled by local operators who know every twist and turn of the river.

Cycling Adventures

The flat roads along the Yulong River are perfect for biking. Rent a bicycle in Yangshuo and explore at your own pace.

Farm-to-Table Dining

Many family-run restaurants serve dishes made with ingredients harvested just steps away. Try beer fish (píjiǔ yú), a local specialty cooked with river fish and Yangshuo’s famous chili.

Final Thoughts

Hiking the Yulong River is more than just a walk—it’s a journey through timeless landscapes where nature and tradition intertwine. Whether you’re crossing ancient bridges, chatting with farmers, or simply soaking in the views, every step reveals a new layer of Guangxi’s beauty. So lace up your boots, grab your camera, and let the Yulong River’s magic unfold before you.
