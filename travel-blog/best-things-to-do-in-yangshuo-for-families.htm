Best Things to Do in Yangshuo for Families

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Yangshuo is a dream destination for families seeking adventure, culture, and unforgettable natural beauty. Nestled in Guangxi province, this landscape of dramatic karst peaks and the serene Li River has captivated travelers for generations. For families, it offers a unique blend of thrilling outdoor activities, immersive cultural experiences, and moments of pure, tranquil relaxation that appeal to all ages. Planning a trip with kids can be daunting, but Yangshuo’s charm lies in its ability to feel both exotic and welcoming. This guide will walk you through the very best family-friendly activities, ensuring your vacation is filled with joy, discovery, and those precious shared memories.

Embracing the Great Outdoors

The heart of any Yangshuo family trip lies in its stunning natural environment. The iconic karst mountains provide a breathtaking backdrop for every activity, inviting you to explore, play, and connect with nature.

A Li River Cruise or Bamboo Raft Adventure

No visit to Yangshuo is complete without getting out on the water. While the full Li River cruise from Guilin is spectacular, with younger children, the shorter, calmer sections from Yangshuo or the nearby Yulong River are more manageable and equally beautiful.

Opt for a motorized bamboo raft on the Li River for a stable and comfortable ride. The whole family can sit back and enjoy the views that are famously depicted on the 20 RMB note. The gentle hum of the motor allows for easy conversation, and the covered rafts offer protection from the sun. For families with older, more adventurous kids, a traditional bamboo raft on the Yulong River is a must. These rafts are poled by local operators and offer a more serene, traditional experience. You’ll float past water buffalo, ancient bridges, and farmers tending their fields. It’s a living postcard and a wonderfully peaceful way to spend an afternoon.

Cycling Through the Countryside

One of the most beloved family activities in Yangshuo is renting bicycles and exploring the flat, paved paths that wind through the valleys. You can easily rent bikes of all sizes, including ones with child seats or tandem attachments.

A classic and easy route is the Ten-Mile Gallery, a scenic road between Yangshuo town and the Moon Hill village. The path is relatively flat and takes you through a stunning corridor of karst peaks, rice paddies, and small villages. You can stop along the way to explore, take photos with water buffalo, or visit a local farmer's house. The pace is entirely up to you, making it perfect for families. The sense of freedom as you pedal through this incredible landscape is an experience your kids will remember forever.

The Magical Light Show: Impression Sanjie Liu

As the sun sets, prepare for a cultural spectacle unlike any other. Impression Sanjie Liu is a breathtaking open-air show directed by the renowned filmmaker Zhang Yimou. Set on the Li River itself, with twelve karst peaks serving as a natural backdrop, the performance is a feast for the senses.

The show features a cast of hundreds of local fishermen and farmers, who use the river as their stage. The combination of dazzling lights, haunting folk music, and graceful movements tells the story of the local Zhuang culture. For children, the sheer scale and visual magic are captivating. It’s not a narrative they need to understand word-for-word; it’s an immersive experience of color, sound, and scale. Be sure to bring a light jacket as it can get cool by the water at night.

Cultural Encounters and Hands-On Fun

Beyond the natural scenery, Yangshuo is a place to connect with a rich cultural heritage. Engaging, hands-on activities make learning fun for kids and adults alike.

A Cooking Class in a Local Farmhouse

Turn your family into master chefs of Guilin cuisine! Several local farms and cooking schools offer family-friendly classes. This isn't just about cooking; it's a full cultural immersion. Often, the experience begins with a visit to a vibrant local market, where your guide will help you select fresh ingredients, explaining the exotic vegetables and spices.

Back in the kitchen, you’ll learn to make classics like Beer Fish (a local Yangshuo specialty), stir-fried dishes, and dumplings. The instructors are typically patient and excellent with children, allowing them to participate in safe tasks like mixing, wrapping, and (with supervision) stirring. Sitting down to a meal that you’ve all helped create is an incredibly rewarding experience and a great way to bring a taste of Yangshuo back home.

Exploring West Street (Xi Jie)

West Street is the bustling, 1,400-year-old heart of Yangshuo. While it's touristy, it's also vibrant, safe, and full of energy that kids love. During the day, it’s a great place to wander, shop for souvenirs, and sample local snacks. Let the kids try sweet candied fruit on a stick or watch artisans make traditional crafts.

At night, the street truly comes alive with colorful lights. While many bars open up, it remains a family-friendly atmosphere for an evening stroll. The street is pedestrianized, so you don't have to worry about traffic. It’s the perfect place to people-watch, enjoy an ice cream, and soak in the international vibe of this historic street.

Trying Your Hand at Chinese Calligraphy or Painting

For a quieter cultural activity, seek out a short calligraphy or painting class. Local artists often open their studios to visitors for introductory lessons. Children will enjoy the tactile experience of grinding the ink, holding the brush, and creating their own characters or simple paintings of the surrounding mountains.

It’s a calming and focused activity that provides a nice balance to the more physical adventures. They get to take home a unique and personal souvenir, and it’s a wonderful way to appreciate the depth of Chinese artistic traditions.

Iconic Sights and Gentle Adventures

Some of Yangshuo's most famous landmarks are also accessible for families, offering a mix of light activity and iconic photo opportunities.

A Visit to Moon Hill

Moon Hill is one of the most recognizable landmarks in Yangshuo. This natural arch at the top of a hill looks like a crescent moon. While hiking to the very top involves a steep climb of about 800 steps, you don't need to go all the way for a fantastic experience.

For families with younger children, simply hiking partway up the path provides stunning views of the surrounding countryside. The effort is well worth it, and you can set your own turnaround point. The path is well-maintained, and there are often vendors selling cold drinks at the base and along the route. It’s a great goal-oriented activity that gives everyone a sense of accomplishment.

Cormorant Fishing Show

While traditional cormorant fishing is no longer a primary industry, it is preserved as a cultural show for visitors. As dusk falls, you can see local fishermen on their bamboo rafts with their trained cormorant birds. The birds dive into the water to catch fish, which are then retrieved by the fishermen.

Children are fascinated by this unique human-animal partnership. The sight of the birds with their wings spread wide to dry, the glow of the lanterns on the dark water, and the skill of the fishermen creates a magical, almost timeless scene. It’s a short but memorable activity, perfect for capping off a day of exploration.

Mud Caves and Water Caves

For the ultimate messy family adventure, consider a trip to one of the area's mud or water caves. These are natural caves where you can slide and play in soft, silky mud baths and then wash off in refreshing underground pools.

It’s an activity that elicits pure, unadulterated joy and laughter from kids (and brave parents!). You’ll be provided with helmets and headlamps, and the experience feels like a real-life adventure. Be sure to wear old swimsuits and bring a full change of clothes. It’s a guaranteed highlight and a fantastic story to tell friends back home.

Practical Tips for a Smooth Family Trip

To ensure your family vacation is as stress-free as possible, a little planning goes a long way.

The best times to visit Yangshuo are in the spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November) when the weather is mild and pleasant. Summers can be hot and humid, while winters are cool and damp.

When it comes to getting around, walking is easy in Yangshuo town. For longer trips, taxis and the ubiquitous "electric buggies" are fun and affordable for families. For the ultimate flexibility, consider hiring a private driver with a van for a day to visit outlying attractions.

Accommodation options are plentiful. You can choose from international chain hotels, charming boutique guesthouses, or for a truly special experience, a family-friendly resort or farmstay in the countryside, which often offers more space for kids to run around.

Embrace the local food, but also know that Yangshuo is very accustomed to international palates. Noodle soups, fried rice, and dumplings are usually a hit with kids. Many restaurants also offer Western-style food if your children need a familiar meal.

Ultimately, the best thing to do in Yangshuo with your family is to slow down and be present. Whether you're marveling at the mountains, sharing a laugh on a bike, or tasting a new dish together, it's these shared moments of wonder that will define your journey.
